A developer working within the TON ecosystem has documented their experience navigating the TON App Center submission process, detailing how an initial rejection led to meaningful improvements in their application.

The submission journey spanned twenty days and involved multiple critical steps. The initial rejection prompted the development team to reassess their approach, identify gaps in their implementation, and make substantial revisions to their project before resubmission.

A significant hurdle involved an overlooked SDK requirement that contributed to the initial rejection. This oversight highlighted the importance of thoroughly reviewing technical documentation and specifications before submission. Once identified, addressing this requirement became a priority in the revision process.

Beyond the technical requirements, the team recognized the need for a complete redesign of certain elements. This suggested that user experience and interface considerations played a role in the review process. The redesign effort consumed considerable time but ultimately strengthened the overall quality of their application.

The experience underscores several lessons for developers building on TON. First, comprehensive documentation review before submission can prevent avoidable rejections. Second, the platform's review process, while selective, appears designed to maintain quality standards across the App Center. Third, rejection should not be viewed as final but rather as constructive feedback that can guide improvement.

The twenty-day timeline between rejection and acceptance demonstrates both the time investment required to build quality applications on TON and the platform's willingness to work with developers committed to meeting its standards.

For builders considering submission to the TON App Center, this case study illustrates that initial setbacks can be overcome through careful attention to technical requirements, willingness to iterate on design, and dedication to meeting platform standards. The journey from rejection to acceptance provides a roadmap for navigating the review process successfully.
