Binance has added TON to its trading pairs against USDT, giving traders on the platform direct access to the Telegram Open Network token through one of crypto's most liquid market venues.

The listing marks another milestone for TON's presence on major centralized exchanges. The trading pair joins existing TON pairs on Binance and other platforms, broadening the routes through which institutional and retail traders can accumulate or exit positions in the token.

TON has maintained steady adoption across the Telegram ecosystem, where it serves as the native token for transaction settlement and validator rewards. The token's integration into Binance's spot trading engine reflects growing institutional confidence in the network's infrastructure and use case.

The pair's availability on Binance—which processes billions in daily volume—ensures competitive pricing and tight spreads for TON/USDT trades. This liquidity improvement can reduce slippage for larger orders and makes the token more accessible to traders using USDT as a base pair.

No details were provided regarding specific trading fees, minimum order sizes, or promotional conditions tied to the listing. Binance regularly lists new pairs as part of its standard operations, and the addition follows the exchange's established evaluation criteria for spot trading eligibility.

TON continues to attract development activity on its blockchain, with projects building across DeFi, NFTs, and decentralized applications. The token's market position remains tied to broader blockchain adoption and Telegram's user expansion.
