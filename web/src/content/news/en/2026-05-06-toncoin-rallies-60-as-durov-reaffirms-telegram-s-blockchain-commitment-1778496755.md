Toncoin experienced a significant price rally, climbing approximately 60% in recent trading. The surge followed comments from Pavel Durov, Telegram's founder, reaffirming the messaging platform's commitment to the TON blockchain project.

Durov's remarks appear to have reassured the market about Telegram's continued backing of TON infrastructure and development. His public defense of the initiative came at a time when investor sentiment around the ecosystem has been closely tied to signals from Telegram leadership.

The timing of the rally suggests market participants view Durov's statements as material to TON's trajectory. Telegram has maintained a complex relationship with TON since its inception—initially championing the blockchain before stepping back amid regulatory pressures, then gradually increasing its integration with the network in recent years through features like TON Wallet and in-app transactions.

Toncoin remains one of the larger cryptocurrencies by market capitalization, and movements of this magnitude reflect the volatility typical of Layer 1 blockchain tokens. The 60% gain represents a notable one-day or short-term move, though the broader context of Durov's comments—whether routine reaffirmation or response to specific doubts—shapes how the market may interpret the strength of Telegram's strategic position in the space.

The rally underscores how closely tied TON's price action remains to sentiment about Telegram's intentions, despite efforts by the TON Foundation and independent developers to position the blockchain as a standalone ecosystem.
