The TON blockchain community is scheduled to hold a governance vote on April 8-9, 2026, to decide the fate of Catchain 2.0, a proposed upgrade to the network's consensus mechanism.

Catchain represents a significant evolution in how TON validators coordinate and finalize blocks. The upgrade aims to address scalability concerns and improve transaction throughput by refining the underlying chain validation architecture that sits beneath the main network.

Governance votes on TON are decided by token holders, with voting weight proportional to Toncoin holdings. The two-day window allows the community sufficient time to cast ballots on whether to adopt the Catchain 2.0 specification.

Details of the specific improvements Catchain 2.0 introduces—whether they focus on latency reduction, validator efficiency, or network resilience—were not immediately clear from available sources. However, consensus layer upgrades typically involve careful coordination, as they affect the foundational rules by which the entire network operates.

TON has historically pursued incremental network improvements through community votes rather than unilateral developer decisions, positioning governance decisions as critical inflection points for the protocol's technical direction.

The outcome of the April vote will signal whether validators and token holders view Catchain 2.0 as a necessary step forward or whether the current consensus architecture remains sufficient for the network's near-term needs.
