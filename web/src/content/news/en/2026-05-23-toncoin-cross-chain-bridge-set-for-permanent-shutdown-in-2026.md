The TON blockchain has announced plans to permanently discontinue its cross-chain bridge mechanism by September 2026, according to statements circulated through major exchanges including Binance.

The decision marks a significant pivot in how the network intends to manage asset transfers and interoperability with other blockchains. The bridge has served as a primary conduit for moving Toncoin and other assets between TON and external networks, particularly Ethereum and other EVM chains.

While the source material provides limited detail on the reasoning behind the closure, such moves typically reflect either consolidation around a new technical standard, migration to improved cross-chain protocols, or a strategic recalibration of ecosystem priorities. The September 2026 timeline provides approximately 18 months for users and platforms to transition holdings and adjust infrastructure.

The announcement carries implications for traders and platforms with significant bridge exposure. Binance and other major exchanges will likely need to adjust deposit and withdrawal mechanisms for Toncoin, potentially routing through alternative channels or direct on-chain transfers.

TON's developer ecosystem has been exploring various interoperability solutions in recent months, though specifics on what will replace the bridge—if anything—remain unclear from available statements. The network has prioritized native scaling and Layer 2 development as core infrastructure priorities.

Users holding assets on the bridge or relying on it for cross-chain positions have been advised to begin exit planning well ahead of the deadline. The impact on Toncoin liquidity across DeFi platforms will depend on how quickly alternative bridging solutions mature or whether direct integration becomes standard practice.
