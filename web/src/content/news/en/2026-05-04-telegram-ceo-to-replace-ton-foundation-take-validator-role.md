Pavel Durov, chief executive of Telegram, has announced plans to dismantle the existing TON Foundation and position Telegram as the primary driving force behind the blockchain's development and operations.

The move represents a significant restructuring of TON's governance framework. Rather than operating under the foundation model that has guided the network since its inception, control and decision-making authority would shift directly to Telegram itself. As part of this transition, Durov indicated that Telegram will also become the network's largest validator, a role that carries substantial influence over consensus and block production.

The TON Foundation has served as the primary steward of the blockchain ecosystem, overseeing development grants, ecosystem support, and strategic direction. Its replacement signals a centralization of authority—at least nominally—under Telegram's operational umbrella.

Durov's announcement comes as TON continues to expand its user base and developer ecosystem, though questions remain about how this governance shift will affect the network's decentralization narrative. Validators and ecosystem participants will likely scrutinize the mechanics of this transition, particularly regarding how decision-making processes will function and what safeguards, if any, will constrain Telegram's unilateral control.

The timing and full implementation details have not yet been disclosed. The blockchain community will be watching closely to see whether this represents a formalization of existing influence or a material shift in how TON operates.
