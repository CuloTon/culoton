Toncoin climbed 24% in recent trading, part of a sharp rally across multiple digital assets as a significant short squeeze began to unwind, according to data from Bitget.

The move came as traders holding leveraged short positions were forced to buy back assets to cover losses, creating upward pressure across the market. A total of roughly $300 million in short positions were liquidated during the event, traders noted.

The rally was broad-based, with Zcash leading the charge at 37% gains. Toncoin's 24% advance placed it among the day's strongest performers, though the exact catalyst for the shift in market sentiment remained tied primarily to the mechanics of the squeeze itself rather than any single news event.

Short squeezes occur when a large number of bearish traders are forced out of positions simultaneously, triggering forced buying that can accelerate price movements beyond what underlying fundamentals alone might suggest. The $300 million liquidation figure underscores the scale of leveraged exposure that unwound in the session.

Such moves highlight the volatility inherent in crypto markets, where leverage and rapid liquidation cascades can amplify single-day swings. For Toncoin, which has drawn interest as part of the broader TON ecosystem developed in association with Telegram, the move represented a sharp but not unprecedented daily movement.

Market observers noted that while short squeezes can create trading opportunities, they also underscore risks for leveraged participants. The speed and magnitude of liquidation events in crypto remain significantly faster than in traditional markets, given the 24/7 nature of digital asset exchanges.
