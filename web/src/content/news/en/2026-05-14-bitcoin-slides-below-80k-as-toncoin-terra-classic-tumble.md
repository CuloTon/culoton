Bitcoin has dropped below the $80,000 mark, extending a pullback that has rippled across major digital assets. The world's largest cryptocurrency by market cap is testing fresh downside pressure, signaling renewed uncertainty in the broader market.

Toncoin and Terra Classic have emerged as the session's steepest decliners, leading a broader retreat in altcoin valuations. The scale of their losses underscores the volatility characteristic of the current market environment, where moves in bitcoin often cascade into sharp repositioning across smaller-cap tokens.

The decline comes as traders reassess risk positioning ahead of key macroeconomic data and potential policy shifts. Bitcoin's failure to hold ground above $80,000 has triggered stop-loss orders and renewed selling pressure, creating a feedback loop that has amplified moves lower.

Toncoin, the native token of the TON blockchain—the protocol underpinning Telegram's infrastructure—has been hit particularly hard. The sell-off suggests that despite ongoing development activity and ecosystem expansion, investor appetite for exposure to Layer 1 alternatives remains sensitive to broader market sentiment.

Terra Classic's renewed weakness adds to the lingering skepticism that continues to shadow the project following its previous collapse. The token's inability to mount sustained rallies reflects persistent doubt among market participants.

The move lower in bitcoin and its ripple effects across altcoins serve as a reminder that macroeconomic headwinds and technical levels remain the dominant forces shaping price action. Whether support holds at current levels will likely determine sentiment for the near term.
