Cryptocurrency markets extended gains into early May, with total capitalization climbing to $2.63 trillion—a $17.14 billion increment from the prior session.

Institutional buying has emerged as the primary catalyst. US spot Bitcoin exchange-traded funds absorbed $573.28 million in a single day on May 1, the largest daily inflow since January. Analysts attribute the sustained demand to a combination of geopolitical relief and institutional confidence following earlier risk-on positioning tied to policy shifts.

Bitcoin traded at $80,405, up marginally at 0.69% for the session. The asset continues to track within an upward channel that has supported prices since late March. On-chain signals, particularly the Chaikin Money Flow indicator, point to steady institutional accumulation rather than speculative chasing.

Toncoin delivered the sharpest move, rallying roughly 30% to $1.744 following Pavel Durov's announcement regarding the TON ecosystem. The Telegram founder disclosed that network transaction fees have been cut by a factor of six, reducing costs toward negligible levels. He also confirmed that Telegram has taken on the role of TON's largest validator, effectively anchoring the platform as a structural pillar of the blockchain.

The fee reduction carries strategic weight given Telegram's 950 million user base, which could theoretically access near-frictionless on-chain micropayments. The move signals a deepening operational commitment from Telegram to TON's infrastructure and governance.

Market participants are tracking whether the sector can hold above the $2.64 trillion threshold. A sustained break would open sightlines toward $2.69 trillion and $2.73 trillion in total market cap. Support sits at $2.58 trillion, with a deeper floor at $2.48 trillion. Bitcoin's technical setup suggests breakout potential above $82,139 could extend toward $86,277.
