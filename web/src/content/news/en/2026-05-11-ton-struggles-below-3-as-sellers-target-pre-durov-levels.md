Toncoin has come under renewed downward pressure as traders position for potential declines toward price levels last seen before the August event that rattled confidence in the asset. The $3 mark, once eyed as a near-term floor by some analysts, has proven elusive despite multiple attempts to stabilize above it.

Selling interest has intensified as bears target support levels from an earlier period in the year—essentially unwinding gains that coincided with or followed Durov's detention and the subsequent media cycle around Telegram's founder. The timing suggests market participants are reassessing their exposure to TON amid broader volatility.

Technical resistance points remain contested. Buyers have struggled to establish meaningful floor-building activity, and the lack of fresh bullish catalysts has allowed shorting pressure to persist. Volume patterns indicate neither conviction nor capitulation—a sign the market may be consolidating before a clearer directional move.

Context matters here: TON's price action has long been entangled with narrative around Telegram and its leadership. The earlier announcement surrounding Durov's legal situation created a macro overhang that some investors are still unwinding. Whether current levels represent capitulation or merely a mid-range correction remains an open question.

On-chain metrics have shown mixed signals. While some long-term accumulation patterns persist, near-term flow data suggests institutional or sophisticated retail participants are de-risking positions. The absence of stabilizing buying from large holders adds to the bearish undertone.
