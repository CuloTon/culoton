Swiss cryptocurrency asset manager 21Shares has rolled out a Toncoin staking exchange-traded product (ETP), now trading on the Frankfurt Stock Exchange under the ticker symbol 2TON.DE. The product provides institutional and retail investors with regulated exposure to Toncoin's staking yields without requiring direct token custody or involvement in blockchain infrastructure.

The ETP structure allows holders to benefit from Toncoin price appreciation while simultaneously earning staking rewards—a dual return mechanism that has become increasingly popular among traditional finance participants entering crypto markets. By wrapping staking functionality within a familiar securities framework, the product lowers technical and operational barriers for conventional investors.

21Shares, which operates under Swiss regulatory oversight, has established itself as a prominent issuer of cryptocurrency-linked ETPs in Europe. The Frankfurt listing represents expansion of its product suite and reinforces growing institutional appetite for Toncoin exposure beyond decentralized platforms.

The Toncoin network, developed by the Telegram team, has seen steadily increasing adoption across the TON ecosystem. Staking mechanisms are core to network security and validator compensation. By tokenizing these yields into a tradable security, 21Shares bridges the gap between TON's blockchain infrastructure and conventional investment channels.

The ETP's appearance on a major European exchange signals continued legitimization of Toncoin as an asset class worthy of institutional infrastructure. Market participants can now gain exposure through traditional brokerage accounts and custodians already integrated with Frankfurt trading systems, removing friction that previously required specialized crypto exchanges or self-custody arrangements.
