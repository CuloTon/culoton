Binance has activated trading for TON/USDT on its spot market, adding another native pair for Toncoin to one of the industry's most liquid platforms.

The listing marks a straightforward expansion of trading infrastructure for TON, the native token of the Telegram-backed TON blockchain. With the pair now live, traders can swap between Toncoin and Tether's stablecoin without intermediary conversions, reducing slippage and execution costs for both retail and institutional participants.

Binance already hosted TON against other major pairs, but the USDT pairing specifically addresses the market's preference for stablecoin-denominated trades. USDT remains the most actively traded stablecoin globally, and its integration with TON improves price discovery and settlement efficiency.

The move aligns with Binance's broader strategy of maintaining comprehensive coverage of high-volume layer-1 ecosystems. TON has attracted significant developer activity and transaction volume since its mainnet integration into the Telegram app, making it a natural fit for the exchange's listing criteria.

No trading fees or promotional incentives were announced in connection with the listing. The pair is available to users in Binance's standard spot trading interface across supported jurisdictions.
