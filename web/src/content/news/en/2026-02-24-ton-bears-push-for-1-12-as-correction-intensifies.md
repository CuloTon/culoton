Toncoin is experiencing a deepening correction as bearish momentum builds across the market. Technical analysis from Mitrade and other trading desks points to $1.12 as a key support level that sellers are actively targeting.

The pullback reflects broader pressure on TON after its earlier gains. Chart watchers note that weakness has accelerated through multiple resistance barriers, with volume patterns consistent with continued downside exploration. The $1.12 level represents a significant technical floor—a break below it would expose deeper support zones and potentially trigger cascading liquidations among leveraged traders.

Market participants are divided on whether this represents a healthy consolidation within a longer-term uptrend or the beginning of a more sustained decline. Sell-side positioning has grown noticeably tighter, and on-chain metrics show mixed signals about institutional accumulation versus distribution at current levels.

Tradable support clusters extend downward from the immediate $1.12 target, with secondary levels positioned further below. The intensity of bear activity suggests this correction may persist unless specific bullish catalysts—whether protocol upgrades, ecosystem adoption milestones, or broader crypto market recovery—emerge to shift sentiment.

As of now, TON trades within a compressed range, with intraday volatility reflecting uncertainty about the next major directional move. Traders watching the pair should monitor volume and price action around the $1.12 zone closely, as a sustained close below that level would signal confirmation of deeper weakness ahead.
