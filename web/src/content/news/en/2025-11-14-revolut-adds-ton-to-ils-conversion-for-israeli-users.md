Revolut has integrated Toncoin (TON) conversion functionality for users in Israel, allowing direct trades between TON and Israeli New Shekels (ILS). The move represents a modest but tangible expansion of TON's accessibility in Middle Eastern markets.

The addition sits within Revolut's broader push to deepen cryptocurrency trading pairs across jurisdictions. For Israeli users, the feature removes a friction point: converting TON now requires fewer intermediary steps, whether for acquisition or liquidation.

TON has steadily expanded its infrastructure and exchange partnerships since 2023, though mainstream fintech adoption has remained patchy. Revolut's move suggests growing confidence that TON liquidity and user demand justify dedicated pairs rather than relying on indirect routing through major stablecoins or fiat gateways.

Israel's crypto-friendly regulatory stance, paired with a substantial tech-savvy user base, makes it a natural market for such expansion. Revolut operates across Europe and select emerging markets; the Israel corridor has become increasingly important for fintech operators seeking geographic diversification.

The conversion pair itself carries no novelty—crypto exchanges routinely add new fiat pairings as market conditions and user demand warrant. But the choice to feature TON alongside major assets signals that Revolut sees sufficient demand to justify the integration effort.

No pricing details or launch date specifics were provided in the announcement. Users in Israel with existing Revolut accounts should have access to the conversion feature through the platform's standard crypto interface.
