Toncoin gained 24% in recent trading, part of a wider market rally driven by a $300 million short squeeze that forced liquidations across major crypto positions.

The surge reflects growing momentum in token markets as traders holding short bets were forced to cover positions at elevated prices, compressing their leverage in the process. The cascade effect pulled a range of assets higher, with Zcash emerging as the session's star performer, climbing 37% on the back of the same liquidation wave.

Short squeezes occur when borrowed positions must be unwound rapidly, often as underlying collateral declines or liquidation thresholds are breached. In this instance, the scale — $300 million in forced closures — was substantial enough to move pricing across multiple tokens.

Toncoin, the native asset of the TON blockchain, has seen renewed attention in recent months as its ecosystem matures and adoption signals improve. The token's connection to Telegram, and by extension the broader Telegram Mini App ecosystem, continues to provide structural support for longer-term narratives around the network.

Market volatility of this magnitude typically subsides within hours or days once positions are fully cleared and order books rebalance. Traders watching the squeeze noted the speed with which liquidation cascades propagated across derivatives platforms, underscoring the interconnected nature of leveraged trading in crypto.

No specific catalyst was cited for the initial trigger of the squeeze. Market participants attributed the move to a combination of technical factors and positioning that had accumulated in short markets ahead of the event.
