Cardano gained 5% in 24 hours to trade above $0.27, riding a wave of market-wide optimism that has accelerated since Bitcoin's climb past $81,000. The move reflects a shift in investor sentiment tied largely to geopolitical developments—specifically a reported pause on certain U.S. economic policies that analysts read as risk-appetite positive, pushing equities and cryptocurrencies higher while weakening the dollar.

ADA's performance sits squarely within a broader altcoin rally. Toncoin and Zcash have surged more than 30% in the past day, while numerous other tokens posted double-digit gains. For Cardano specifically, weekly gains stand at 8%, with the token decisively clearing the $0.25 support level.

Derivatives data underscores trader conviction. Open interest in ADA futures has climbed to $546 million, while positive funding rates for perpetual contracts—at 0.0074%—signal continued long positioning. Spot trading volume reached $129 million in 24 hours, indicating solid participation across venues.

Technically, the setup favors further upside. Cardano has broken above its 50-day exponential moving average at $0.25 and appears poised to test a descending triangle breakout pattern. The Relative Strength Index sits at 66—bullish territory without yet entering overbought conditions, leaving room for additional movement before any momentum exhaustion.

Near-term resistance clusters at $0.30, marked by a horizontal line from March highs. If that clears, the 200-day EMA near $0.40 becomes the next substantive hurdle, with $0.50 potentially in scope if buying pressure sustains. Support zones should the mood darken rest at $0.25 and $0.23; a close below $0.23 would risk bringing $0.20 into focus.

The question now is whether this round of risk appetite proves durable or represents a temporary reprieve. Cardano's charts suggest room for higher levels, but macro conditions remain the ultimate driver.
