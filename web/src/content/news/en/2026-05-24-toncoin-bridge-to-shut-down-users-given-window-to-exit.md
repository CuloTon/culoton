A significant bridge infrastructure component in the TON blockchain ecosystem is being decommissioned, according to confirmed reports. The shutdown affects users who have assets locked or in transit through the service, creating an immediate need for fund recovery before the deadline passes.

Bridge services serve as connectors between different blockchain networks, allowing users to move assets across chains. The discontinuation of this particular bridge represents a notable shift in TON's infrastructure landscape, particularly for those who relied on it for cross-chain liquidity or asset transfers.

Users have been notified of a specific deadline by which they must withdraw their funds. Failure to act within this window risks leaving assets inaccessible, a scenario that has played out in previous bridge closures across the broader crypto ecosystem. The exact timeline and technical process for withdrawal have been communicated, though the precise mechanisms vary depending on which assets or positions are involved.

The reasons cited for the shutdown were not immediately clear from available information, though bridge closures typically reflect operational decisions, consolidation of services, or shifts in business strategy. It remains unclear whether the bridge operator is directing users toward alternative solutions or if they are expected to independently source replacements.

For the TON community, this development underscores the ongoing evolution of the ecosystem's infrastructure layer. Bridges have been a focal point of both innovation and risk in crypto over the past two years, with several high-profile exploits and service disruptions affecting user confidence. Whether this particular closure was driven by technical, commercial, or risk management considerations has not been specified.

Users with assets held on the bridge are advised to prioritize withdrawal well ahead of any announced deadline to avoid potential complications.
