Toncoin rallied sharply on Wednesday, gaining 24% in a broader market movement that saw significant liquidations across the crypto sector, according to trading data from Bitget.

The surge in TON coincided with a $300 million short squeeze that rippled through major digital assets. Short squeezes occur when investors holding bearish positions are forced to buy back tokens to cover losses, typically after price moves against their wagers. The momentum pushed several cryptocurrencies into the green, with Zcash posting the day's strongest performance at 37%.

Market participants attributed the move to a combination of technical factors and reduced selling pressure. TON's 24% gain placed it among the session's top performers, reflecting investor appetite for exposure to the Telegram-connected blockchain ecosystem.

The liquidation cascade highlighted the leverage built into current market positions. Bitget's data showed the squeeze affected positions across multiple trading pairs and venues, suggesting broad positioning adjustments rather than isolated movement in a single asset.

Toncoin's performance reflects persistent retail and institutional interest in the TON network, which has continued attracting developers and applications despite broader market volatility. Whether the move represents a sustained shift in sentiment or a technical correction remains unclear; historical short squeezes often produce sharp reversals rather than sustainable trends.

Traders monitoring leverage ratios across exchanges noted elevated short interest had preceded the move, setting conditions for the eventual unwind. Such events underscore the risks posed by excessive borrowed positions in volatile markets.
