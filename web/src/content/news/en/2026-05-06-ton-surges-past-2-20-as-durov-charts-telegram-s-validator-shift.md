Toncoin reached $2.215 on Wednesday, marking its strongest three-day performance this year and the highest price since mid-November. The rally followed a pair of statements from Telegram founder Pavel Durov outlining the messaging platform's expanded role in The Open Network's infrastructure.

Durov's initial May 4 post announced that Telegram would replace the TON Foundation as the network's primary operational driver and assume the position of largest validator. He also cited a sixfold reduction in transaction fees and promised infrastructure upgrades within two to three weeks. A follow-up post the next day addressed skepticism about concentration of power, arguing that Telegram's validator position would paradoxically strengthen decentralization by serving as a counterbalance to other major players and encouraging wider participation.

Market data showed trading activity concentrated in the $2.10–$2.20 band across major venues, with Kraken and OKX both reporting highs near $2.215. The move remains substantial relative to recent price action but represents only a fraction of TON's prior all-time high near $8.25.

The timing and sequencing of Durov's announcements shaped market interpretation. Rather than generic ecosystem news, the narrative centered on a specific Telegram-led roadmap: lower fees, improved developer tooling, upgraded infrastructure, and a validator shift that tightens the network's connection to Telegram's user base and distribution.

The transition raises a structural question about governance risk. In a proof-of-stake system, the largest validator holds outsized influence over network security and operations. TON's original design as the Telegram Open Network had evolved toward a more independent foundation-led structure; this reversal marks a material shift in how investors assess the network's autonomy and execution risk.

At time of writing, TON traded at $2.263.
