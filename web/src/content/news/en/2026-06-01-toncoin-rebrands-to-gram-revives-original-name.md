Pavel Durov said on June 1 that TON's native coin would be renamed from Toncoin to Gram, reviving the label the token carried in the network's first white paper back in 2018. The Telegram founder framed the move as a return to origins and the fourth step of his "Make TON Great Again" roadmap, a seven-part plan that has driven the project's narrative since the spring.

The change is largely cosmetic in mechanical terms. Durov said the transition would take roughly three weeks, that the blockchain itself would keep the TON name, and that ownership records, staking positions and network operations would be unaffected. A fresh landing page at gram.org went live alongside the announcement, signalling that the rebrand is already in motion rather than merely under discussion.

What gives the name weight is its history. Gram was the original token in Telegram's 2018 blockchain plan, backed by a private sale that raised roughly $1.7 billion — among the largest crypto raises of its era. The U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission sued Telegram in October 2019, treating Gram as an unregistered security, and the case settled in mid-2020 with the company returning about $1.22 billion to investors and paying an $18.5 million penalty. Telegram walked away from the project; an independent community later revived the network and renamed the coin Toncoin. Bringing back "Gram" closes that loop, six years on.

Markets read the gesture as bullish. TON climbed roughly 15% in the hours after the post, with trading volume up around 135%, extending a rally that has run since Telegram moved to retake control of the network and become its largest validator earlier in the spring. Asian desks tracked the move closely, with Korean outlets reporting a multi-day surge as the rebrand landed on top of the broader Telegram-integration story.

Whether a name carries the project further is a separate question from whether it trades higher this week. The rebrand changes branding, not throughput, liquidity or the thin on-chain depth analysts have flagged repeatedly. Still, for a token whose entire arc has been tied to Telegram's ambitions, reclaiming the name it was born with is a clear statement of intent — and a reminder that the next chapters of the roadmap, from TON Pay to the Bitcoin bridge, are the ones that will have to do the heavier lifting.
