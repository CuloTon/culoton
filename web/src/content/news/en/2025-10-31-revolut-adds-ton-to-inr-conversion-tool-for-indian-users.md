Revolut has integrated Toncoin (TON) pricing data into its currency conversion interface, allowing users in India to check real-time exchange rates between TON and the Indian rupee.

The addition reflects growing demand for cryptocurrency access among Indian retail users and expands Revolut's coverage of alternative assets beyond traditional forex and commodities. The fintech platform, which operates across multiple markets with varying regulatory frameworks, has been gradually broadening its crypto offerings in regions where licensing permits.

India's cryptocurrency market remains fragmented by regulatory uncertainty at the federal level, though state-level fintech adoption has accelerated. Revolut's move to surface TON pricing suggests confidence in sustained interest among the platform's existing Indian user base, which has grown substantially since the company expanded operations to the subcontinent.

TON, the blockchain powering Telegram's ecosystem, has maintained significant trading volumes across major exchanges despite periodic volatility. The token trades across centralized exchanges and decentralized platforms, with INR pairs increasingly available through regional trading venues.

The tool itself appears to be a straightforward pricing integration rather than a new trading product, allowing users to monitor TON valuation against rupees without necessarily executing trades through Revolut itself. Such conversion utilities have become standard across fintech platforms as a baseline feature for cryptocurrency-curious users.

Revolut's decision to highlight this functionality comes as established fintech players continue calibrating their crypto strategies across jurisdictions with differing regulatory appetites for digital assets.
