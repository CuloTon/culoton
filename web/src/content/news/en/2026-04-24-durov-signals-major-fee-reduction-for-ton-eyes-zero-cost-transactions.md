Pavel Durov, founder of Telegram and architect of the TON blockchain, has announced a substantial reduction in network transaction costs. The confirmed measure amounts to a sixfold decrease in fees—a move aimed at lowering barriers to network participation and improving user experience across the ecosystem.

The fee reduction represents a deliberate effort to make TON more competitive among Layer 1 blockchains, many of which already offer low-cost or minimal-fee transactions. Current fees, while reasonable by some standards, have remained a friction point for routine interactions and smaller transfers.

Beyond the immediate sixfold cut, Durov has indicated that the long-term roadmap includes moving toward completely feeless transactions. This represents an even more ambitious goal and suggests the development team is exploring mechanisms to subsidize or eliminate transaction costs through alternative funding models or network efficiency improvements.

The timing of this announcement comes as TON continues to expand its developer ecosystem and user base. Reduced fees could accelerate adoption among retail users and developers building on the platform, particularly for applications where transaction costs were previously prohibitive.

Implementation details and timeline for both the immediate fee reduction and the zero-fee phase have not yet been disclosed. The practical feasibility of completely feeless transactions typically depends on network security models, spam prevention mechanisms, and how costs are distributed across ecosystem participants.

TON's fee structure has been competitive relative to other major blockchains, but the Ethereum network's high gas costs and congestion on other chains have highlighted the advantage of low-friction networks. This move positions TON more aggressively in that competitive landscape.

The announcement underscores Durov's stated commitment to making TON a viable platform for mass adoption, rather than a niche asset for speculators. Whether feeless transactions become reality will depend on technical implementation and the broader evolution of blockchain economics.
