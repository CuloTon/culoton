The Gram rebrand took the headlines this week, but it is only step four of Pavel Durov's seven-part "Make TON Great Again" plan — and arguably the easiest one. The steps already shipped are the structural ones: the Catchain 2.0 upgrade in April brought block finality to roughly 0.6 seconds, fees were cut about sixfold to fractions of a cent, and on May 4 Telegram replaced the TON Foundation as the network's largest validator. With control consolidated, the roadmap now turns to features users actually touch.

Next up is TON Pay 2.0, slated for this quarter. It builds on the TON Pay SDK launched in February, which lets Telegram Mini Apps accept Toncoin and USDT, and pushes toward instant in-app payments at a sub-$0.0005 cost per transaction. The planned additions are the ones that matter for real commerce: subscriptions for recurring charges, gasless transactions where merchants sponsor the network fee, and fiat off-ramps to convert crypto revenue into traditional currency. The ambition is to turn Telegram's roughly one billion users into a native checkout layer.

After payments comes TON Teleport, targeted for mid-2026 — a cross-chain bridge built to bring Bitcoin liquidity into the ecosystem, letting BTC holders put their coins to work in TON-based DeFi without selling into TON first. A Telegram Stars expansion is pencilled in for the third quarter, aimed at driving everyday transaction volume, with further development phases sketched out through 2026 and 2027.

These are the steps that change usage rather than branding. Analysts have flagged the same weak spot repeatedly — thin on-chain liquidity and shallow order-book depth — and payments plus a Bitcoin bridge are precisely how TON would translate Telegram's reach into volume that shows up on the chain. The plan is coherent; the open question is execution, and each of these phases carries more technical and regulatory risk than swapping a name on a token.
