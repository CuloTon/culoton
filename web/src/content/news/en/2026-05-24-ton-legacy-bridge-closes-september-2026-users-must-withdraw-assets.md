The Open Network's legacy Token Bridge will cease operations on September 1, 2026, according to an announcement from the TON Foundation. Users holding any bridged assets must withdraw them before that date or risk permanent loss of access.

The shutdown affects two categories of holdings. Those with Wrapped Toncoin on Ethereum or BNB Chain must bridge their tokens back to the TON network via bridge-v3.ton.org. Holders of j-tokens—jUSDT, jUSDC, jDAI, and jWBTC—stored in TON wallets must return them to Ethereum through the same bridge.

To ease the transition, the TON Foundation has waived all percentage-based transfer fees for the remaining withdrawal window. The foundation also confirmed that previously submitted transfers have already been processed and covered fees for any unclaimed withdrawals, removing some friction from the exit process.

The shutdown will unfold in phases. Bridge oracles are scheduled to withdraw their staked TON in June 2026, signaling the first stage of the infrastructure wind-down. Oracles will continue processing transfers until the final deadline, giving users roughly a three-month window to act once that phase begins.

The bridge retirement marks a transition in TON's infrastructure strategy. The ecosystem has expanded its native DeFi capabilities substantially, reducing reliance on legacy cross-chain solutions. This shift follows a period of sustained network growth, partly driven by Telegram's involvement in TON governance and recent protocol upgrades.

Users are advised to check their wallet holdings immediately and initiate withdrawals well ahead of September to account for potential network delays. The foundation has emphasized the importance of timely action as the network transitions to newer cross-chain infrastructure.
