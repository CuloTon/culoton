Chainlink has extended its Cross-Chain Interoperability Protocol (CCIP) and Data Streams services to the TON blockchain, marking a significant step toward integrating TON into the broader multi-chain ecosystem.

The expansion grants TON access to Chainlink's established infrastructure for secure cross-chain communication and reliable price feeds. CCIP enables applications on TON to interact seamlessly with smart contracts and liquidity pools across other major blockchains, while Data Streams provides verified on-chain and off-chain data for dApps built on the network.

For developers and projects on TON, the integration removes a critical barrier to interoperability. Applications can now execute cross-chain transfers, access collateral locked on other networks, and tap into liquidity pools without building bespoke bridge solutions. The canonical designation suggests Chainlink's infrastructure will be the standard pathway for TON's cross-chain transactions, reducing fragmentation and security risks that often accompany ad-hoc bridging solutions.

TON has grown rapidly as a blockchain platform, particularly through Telegram integration and a strong developer community. However, cross-chain connectivity has historically been limited compared to established ecosystems like Ethereum or Solana. This partnership directly addresses that gap, potentially unlocking new use cases in decentralized finance, gaming, and enterprise applications that require assets and data from multiple chains.

The move also signals Chainlink's commitment to expanding beyond the traditional layer-1 hierarchy. By establishing TON as a canonical endpoint within its network, Chainlink reinforces its role as blockchain infrastructure rather than a single-chain oracle service.

No details were provided regarding launch timing or specific feature rollout phases.
