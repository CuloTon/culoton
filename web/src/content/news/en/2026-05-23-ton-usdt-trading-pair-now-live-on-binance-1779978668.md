Binance has activated trading for TON against USDT, the stablecoin pair, on its spot exchange. The listing marks an expansion of TON liquidity options on one of the world's largest cryptocurrency exchanges.

TON, the native token of the TON blockchain developed by Telegram, has steadily integrated into major trading venues since its initial launch. The addition of a direct USDT pair provides traders with a stablecoin denominated route to buy and sell the asset, a standard offering for established tokens seeking to reduce volatility in order entry and exit.

Binance's move reflects growing institutional and retail interest in the TON ecosystem, particularly as the blockchain has attracted developer activity and on-chain applications since Telegram's backing became public knowledge. The exchange had previously listed TON/BUSD, Binance's own stablecoin, but the introduction of a USDT pair widens access for traders who prefer working with Tether's stablecoin infrastructure.

Trading pairs involving major stablecoins typically see higher volumes and tighter spreads, benefiting both market makers and ordinary traders. The move is consistent with Binance's strategy of supporting tokens with demonstrated ecosystem depth and community engagement.

No official announcement regarding trading fees, minimum order sizes, or promotional incentives was immediately available from Binance at publication time.
