TON's price rose sharply this week, gaining roughly 30% after Telegram announced a structural shift in how the TON Foundation operates within the ecosystem.

Under the new arrangement, Telegram is moving the foundation toward a stewardship model, stepping back from direct operational control. The shift signals a move toward greater decentralization, with the foundation taking on a more advisory posture rather than active day-to-day governance of the protocol.

Market participants interpreted the announcement as positive. A more distributed power structure has long been a stated goal within blockchain communities, and TON's move aligns with broader efforts to reduce centralization concerns that have shadowed the project since its origins at Telegram.

The foundation's reduced role does not diminish Telegram's broader influence over the ecosystem—the platform remains central to TON adoption and use cases. However, the formal repositioning creates clearer separation between Telegram's commercial interests and the protocol's governance layer, a distinction that can matter to investors and developers assessing long-term resilience.

TON has been expanding its ecosystem aggressively, with growing activity in decentralized finance, NFTs, and gaming applications. The governance restructuring comes at a time when the network is competing for developer attention and institutional interest against established chains and newer competitors.

The price movement reflects confidence that the framework change reduces regulatory and structural risks—both perennial concerns for projects with strong founding company ties. Whether the foundation's advisory model translates into sustained protocol progress will depend on execution by community developers and the broader TON ecosystem.
