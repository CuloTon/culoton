Bitcoin has dipped below the $80,000 mark, marking a fresh retreat for the market leader after weeks of elevated trading levels. The move signals renewed selling pressure across major cryptocurrency holdings, though the broader market remains volatile.

Toncoin has emerged as one of the session's notable underperformers, trading down alongside Terra Classic in a coordinated wave of profit-taking that has rippled through mid-cap and large-cap tokens alike. The dual downturn suggests traders are reassessing positions across multiple chains and use cases.

While Bitcoin's fall below $80,000 represents a technical retreat, the magnitude of declines in alternative tokens like Toncoin points to sector-wide rotation rather than isolated weakness. Trading volumes remain elevated, indicating active participation in the selloff rather than thin-market mechanics.

The decline follows a period during which Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies had climbed toward resistance levels. Market participants are watching to see whether the current levels attract fresh buying interest or whether selling pressure extends further. Toncoin's performance will be worth monitoring as an indicator of broader sentiment toward mid-tier ecosystem tokens.
