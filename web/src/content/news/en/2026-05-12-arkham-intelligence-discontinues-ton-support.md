Arkham Intelligence, one of the leading blockchain analysis platforms, has discontinued support for the TON ecosystem, the company confirmed in recent announcements. The move marks a notable withdrawal from coverage of a network that has seen substantial growth in both adoption and market value.

The timing coincides with Toncoin trading near its highest levels of the year, suggesting the decision is not driven by network weakness or declining user interest. TON has continued to expand its developer base and transaction volume despite the exit of the analytics provider.

Arkham's decision to drop TON coverage represents a reversal of its earlier commitment to track activity across the Telegram-backed blockchain. The firm, which monitors transactions and on-chain behavior across multiple networks, cited undisclosed operational or strategic reasons for the shift.

For users and institutions relying on Arkham's intelligence tools, the move necessitates alternatives for monitoring TON-based activity and wallet behavior. Other blockchain analytics platforms continue to offer coverage of the network, though Arkham's departure removes one significant data provider from the ecosystem.

The disconnect between Arkham's withdrawal and TON's market performance underscores the occasional misalignment between infrastructure decisions and network fundamentals. Whether the move reflects broader internal priorities at Arkham or specific technical constraints remains unclear. What is evident is that TON's continued momentum in price and usage suggests the network is not losing traction among its core participant base.
