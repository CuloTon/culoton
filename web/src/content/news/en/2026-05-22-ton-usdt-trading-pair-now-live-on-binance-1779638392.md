Binance, the world's largest cryptocurrency exchange by volume, has activated trading for TON against USDT. The pairing allows users to buy and sell Toncoin—the native token of the TON blockchain, originally developed by Telegram—using the Tether stablecoin.

The listing represents a significant expansion of TON's market infrastructure, as Binance's scale and liquidity make it a primary venue for institutional and retail traders seeking exposure to the asset. TON has steadily gained prominence in the broader crypto ecosystem since Telegram's investment in its development, with the blockchain now supporting a growing array of decentralized applications and services.

Direct USDT pairs are standard practice for major cryptocurrencies, as they provide traders with a stable reference point and reduce friction in executing positions. The addition of this pairing on Binance's spot market suggests confidence in TON's maturity as a tradable asset.

TON has faced regulatory scrutiny over its association with Telegram, particularly in relation to the messenger's own cryptocurrency ambitions several years ago. However, the blockchain has evolved independently and now operates as a public, permissionless network. Its integration on major exchange venues reflects gradual acceptance of the project within traditional trading infrastructure.

The exact timing and any promotional terms associated with the listing were not specified in the announcement.
