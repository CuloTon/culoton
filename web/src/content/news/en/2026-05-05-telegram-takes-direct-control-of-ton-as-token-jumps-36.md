Toncoin climbed 36% this week following news that Telegram has taken direct operational control of the TON blockchain, displacing the TON Foundation that had overseen the network since its inception.

The shift marks a significant governance change for the ecosystem. Telegram, which created TON before facing regulatory pressure from US authorities, has now reasserted central management of the project. The company simultaneously announced a reduction in transaction fees to near-zero levels, a move apparently aimed at improving user experience and network adoption.

The fee restructuring represents a substantial operational shift. TON's transaction model had previously charged users measurable costs for on-chain activity. Moving fees toward zero aligns with Telegram's broader strategy to integrate blockchain functionality into its messaging platform as a transparent, frictionless layer.

The Foundation's departure from active governance leaves questions about the network's decentralization posture. TON has long occupied an unusual position in crypto: nominally independent, yet closely tied to Telegram's commercial interests and regulatory compliance obligations. The Foundation's replacement by direct Telegram stewardship removes a layer of structural separation.

Market reaction was swift and positive. The 36% surge suggests investor confidence in Telegram's commitment to the network and optimism around reduced barriers to transaction volume. The fee cuts could materially alter network economics, potentially increasing throughput and daily active users if adoption follows.

Telegram's move comes as the company navigates its own regulatory environment and pushes deeper into blockchain-native features for its 900+ million user base. Whether the centralization of control benefits or harms TON's long-term positioning in a competitive L1 landscape remains an open question for participants.
