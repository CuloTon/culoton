XRP has gained 5.7% over the past month, a performance that trails both established peers and smaller assets like Toncoin and Internet Computer. The relative weakness comes even as broader altcoin markets have recovered roughly $125 billion from a deeper drawdown, according to on-chain data reviewed by analyst Darkfost.

What's notable is the persistence of bearish sentiment in XRP derivatives markets. On Binance, funding rates have maintained a short bias for nearly three consecutive months—the longest such stretch in recent history. This occurs despite XRP climbing 27% over the same period, a typical catalyst that would normally shift trader positioning toward bullish.

Darkfost points to this disconnect as a potential inflection point. Historical precedent offers some support: in April 2025, a similar pattern of extreme bearish consensus preceded a sharp reversal that eventually delivered a 126% gain. The analyst notes that such sustained misalignment between price strength and funding sentiment often signals underlying pressure building for a directional break.

On the technical front, interpretations diverge. One analyst observes structural similarities between XRP's current setup and the bear market compression phases that preceded breakouts in Toncoin and Ondo, suggesting XRP could follow suit with a move toward $2–$4.

Others focus on XRP's symmetrical triangle pattern, a formation that signals indecision without predicting direction. The key variable is proximity to the triangle's apex, expected near end-May. An upside break could unlock momentum; a failure would likely invite deeper selling.

The broader context remains fluid. XRP's next move will depend on whether buyers can sustain pressure against overhead resistance while funding rates remain tilted toward shorts. The approaching compression apex suggests a decisive resolution is imminent, though market timing remains uncertain.
