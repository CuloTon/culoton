Pavel Durov, founder of Telegram and TON, has publicly confirmed that the blockchain is moving toward a significant reduction in transaction costs. The first phase will deliver a sixfold decrease in fees, with plans to eventually eliminate fees entirely.

The announcement marks a shift in TON's approach to user accessibility and cost efficiency. High transaction fees have been a persistent friction point across blockchains, and TON's commitment to reducing this barrier suggests the network is prioritizing onboarding and usability.

Durov did not provide a specific timeline for either milestone, though the emphasis on a phased approach—first the fee reduction, then feeless transactions—suggests both developments are within the team's technical roadmap. The distinction between the two targets may indicate different technical implementations or network capacity milestones needed to support fully gasless transactions.

The move aligns with TON's broader strategy of competing as a user-friendly blockchain infrastructure. With Telegram's massive user base as a potential entry point, lower barriers to transaction costs could significantly reduce friction for new participants entering the ecosystem.

Fee structures have become a competitive factor among blockchains, particularly as Ethereum and other Layer 1 networks have faced congestion-driven cost spikes. TON's commitment to this roadmap suggests the network is attempting to position itself as a cost-efficient alternative for high-volume, everyday transactions.

No technical details were provided regarding how feeless transactions would be sustained economically or what changes to the network's consensus mechanism might be required. The statement appears to function primarily as a directional commitment rather than a technical specification.
