Two weeks ago the Gram rebrand looked like a catalyst; this week it looks like a headline. TON spiked almost 20% toward $2.27 on the June 1 announcement, but the entire move has since unwound. The token now trades around $1.60–$1.66 with 24-hour volume near $150 million, lower on the day and back to roughly where it sat before Durov's post. It is a textbook sell-the-news fade.

The reversal fits the fundamentals, or the lack of them. As analysts flagged from the start, a name change moves sentiment, not throughput, fees, or liquidity. With the governance vote now settled and the June 15 ticker switch a known quantity, the speculative reason to hold ahead of the event has evaporated, and thin order-book depth has let price round-trip quickly in both directions.

Technically, TON is parked on support near $1.60, with the $2.80–$3.00 band still the first serious overhead resistance left over from the spring. The next real catalyst is execution, not branding: TON Pay 2.0 turning into payments people actually make, and the TON Teleport Bitcoin bridge bringing genuine liquidity. Until usage shows up on-chain, Gram remains a better story than it is a trade.
