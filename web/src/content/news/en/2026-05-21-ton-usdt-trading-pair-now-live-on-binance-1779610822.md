Binance has activated trading for TON/USDT, allowing users to trade Toncoin directly against Tether on the world's largest cryptocurrency exchange by volume.

The listing represents a straightforward expansion of TON's trading infrastructure on Binance, where the asset already trades against Bitcoin and Ethereum. The USDT pair provides traders with a stablecoin conversion option, potentially reducing slippage for positions that don't require intermediate trading steps.

TON, the native token of the TON blockchain originally developed by Telegram before its pivot to community-driven development, has maintained a position among the larger-cap cryptocurrencies. Binance's pair additions typically reflect both user demand and the exchange's assessment of an asset's trading volume and liquidity profile.

The move comes as TON continues development of its ecosystem, including work on smart contracts, decentralized finance applications, and integration with Telegram's 700+ million user base. Earlier this year, the token saw significant volatility alongside broader crypto market movements, though it has retained substantial trading volumes across multiple venues.

For traders, the addition of USDT pair reduces reliance on intermediate conversions and may tighten spreads for TON/stablecoin trades. The pair joins a growing number of direct stablecoin pairs across major exchanges, reflecting market preference for reduced trading friction.
