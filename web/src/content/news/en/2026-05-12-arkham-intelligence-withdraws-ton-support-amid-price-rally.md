Arkham Intelligence, one of the blockchain industry's most widely used analytics platforms, has ceased support for the TON network, according to reporting from MEXC Exchange. The move comes as Toncoin registered fresh yearly peaks in market value.

Arkham's decision marks a notable withdrawal from the ecosystem at a moment when TON has gained considerable momentum. The timing underscores growing tensions between major analytics providers and certain blockchain protocols over data access, methodology, or commercial terms.

Arkham has built its reputation as a critical infrastructure player, offering on-chain intelligence to traders, institutions, and researchers across multiple blockchains. Its coverage gaps typically signal either technical complexity, resource constraints, or contractual disputes that prevent continued support.

TON, the blockchain backed by Telegram's infrastructure and community, has expanded its ecosystem significantly over the past year. Toncoin's price performance reflects broader appetite for non-Ethereum L1 alternatives, though the network remains smaller by many metrics than established peers.

The analytics firm's departure may limit transparency tools available to TON participants, particularly those relying on Arkham for portfolio tracking, transaction analysis, or market monitoring. Other platforms continue to serve the TON ecosystem, but Arkham's exit reduces competitive optionality in a space where data quality and breadth of coverage remain critical.

Neither Arkham nor the TON Foundation has publicly detailed the reasoning behind the move. Questions about whether this represents a temporary technical issue or permanent strategic decision remain unanswered. For TON stakeholders, the development underscores the ongoing dependency on third-party infrastructure providers whose priorities may shift independently of network growth.
