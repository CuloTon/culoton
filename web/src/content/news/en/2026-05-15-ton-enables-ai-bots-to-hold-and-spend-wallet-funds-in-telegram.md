The Open Network's infrastructure team has introduced a new standard for what it calls Agentic Wallets — a framework that gives Telegram-based AI bots the ability to hold and manage cryptocurrency on behalf of users within clearly defined spending limits.

Under the standard, bots operating in Telegram can now function as semi-autonomous financial agents. Users fund these wallets, and the bots operate within strict parameters set at the outset. The mechanism turns conversational interfaces into direct payment channels, where bot actions — whether purchasing services, facilitating transactions, or executing smart contract interactions — occur natively within the chat environment.

The move reflects broader industry momentum toward on-chain agents: software systems that can execute financial decisions with minimal human intervention per transaction, though within guardrails. For Telegram's 900+ million users, many of whom interact daily with bots for tasks ranging from trading to content curation, the development potentially lowers friction for cryptocurrency adoption.

TON Tech positioned the standard as a response to growing demand for seamless bot-to-chain integration. Agentic Wallets sit between full self-custody and centralized intermediaries, offering users control over spending caps while reducing the user experience friction of approving every small transaction manually.

The technical architecture requires bots to operate under auditable constraints, preventing unauthorized or excessive spending. Whether this becomes a significant adoption vector depends partly on ecosystem developer adoption and regulatory reception — agent-based financial systems remain a relatively novel regulatory terrain in most jurisdictions.

No immediate rollout timeline or integration metrics were disclosed, though the framework is available for developers building on TON now.
