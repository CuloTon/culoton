Toncoin faces near-term downward pressure, with analysts mapping out potential support zones that could arrest the decline.

According to technical assessments, the token's pullback may stabilize somewhere in the $1.12 to $2.01 range. The $2.01 level represents the upper boundary of this correction window, while $1.12 marks a lower threshold. Market participants are watching these levels for signs of sustained buying interest.

The analysis reflects common patterns in cryptocurrency price movement, where pullbacks from gains often encounter support at prior levels or moving averages. In Toncoin's case, the intermediate range between the two support levels could offer natural resting points for the asset.

Technical traders typically use such zones to identify entry points for positions or to set stop-loss levels. The identification of these bands helps market participants prepare for different scenarios—either a bounce from the higher support or a deeper test of lower levels.

Whether Toncoin actually reaches these targets depends on broader market conditions, trading volume, and sentiment toward the TON ecosystem. The coming weeks will likely determine if the pullback proves temporary or signals a more prolonged consolidation phase. For now, the range-bound framework provides a basic roadmap for near-term price action, though crypto markets remain inherently volatile and unpredictable.
