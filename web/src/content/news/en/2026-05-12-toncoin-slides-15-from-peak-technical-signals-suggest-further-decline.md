Toncoin has fallen 15 percent from its recent peak of $2.90, prompting analysis of whether the pullback will deepen further. Technical indicators suggest the correction may continue from current levels.

The move represents a notable shift in momentum for the asset, which had climbed toward the psychological $3.00 mark before encountering selling pressure. Multiple timeframe charts signal weakness that could extend the downside in the near term.

Resistance levels and support zones are being closely watched by traders. The broader question is whether this constitutes a healthy consolidation within an uptrend or the start of a more sustained correction. Market sentiment has cooled somewhat as the pullback gained traction, with volume metrics providing mixed signals about the conviction behind recent selling.

On-chain metrics and funding rates across major exchanges offer additional context for the directional bias. Some analysts point to overbought conditions on certain timeframes as justification for the retreat, while others highlight profit-taking after the asset's prior rally.

The next critical level for support will determine whether bulls can mount a defense or if bears extend control lower. Trading volumes and price action at key zones will be instrumental in clarifying the path forward. For now, the correction remains within the bounds of normal market behavior, though technical evidence suggests caution for buyers until clearer reversal signals emerge.
