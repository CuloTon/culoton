Binance has activated trading for TON against USDT, the stablecoin pair joining the exchange's growing roster of TON trading options.

The listing marks a straightforward addition to spot markets on one of the world's largest cryptocurrency exchanges. TON, the blockchain developed by Telegram, has steadily expanded its exchange presence since mainnet launch, and USDT pairing provides traders with direct stablecoin access to the asset.

Binance previously offered TON trading against other pairs, but the USDT addition gives users a conventional entry and exit route without requiring conversion through intermediate trading pairs. Stablecoin pairs typically see higher trading volume and tighter spreads, particularly for tokens establishing themselves across multiple venues.

The move reflects broader institutional and retail interest in TON as it develops its ecosystem. The network has attracted developer activity and decentralized finance protocols since going live, competing for attention within the crowded layer-one blockchain space.

For Binance, the listing represents routine exchange operations—adding liquidity pairs based on user demand and strategic considerations. The platform has maintained comprehensive TON coverage as the token has grown.
