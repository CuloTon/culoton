Binance has added TON/USDT to its trading pairs, allowing users to trade Toncoin directly against the stablecoin on the world's largest cryptocurrency exchange by volume.

The listing expands liquidity and accessibility for TON, which powers the TON blockchain — an L1 network developed alongside Telegram. The USDT pairing is among the most heavily traded against major cryptocurrencies, providing a direct on-ramp for both retail and institutional traders seeking exposure to the token.

Binance's support underscores institutional appetite for TON as it competes in the crowded L1 space. The exchange remains a critical distribution point for blockchain projects seeking broader market reach and trading volume. Toncoin has maintained significant adoption within Telegram's ecosystem, where it functions as the native asset for transactions and smart contract execution.

The precise trading mechanics, fee structures, and any promotional periods associated with the listing were not disclosed in the announcement. Trading pairs on major exchanges typically launch with standard maker and taker fees unless promotional conditions apply during early trading windows.

TON's presence on Binance spans multiple trading pairs; the USDT addition rounds out options for traders with different liquidity and settlement preferences. The broader trend reflects a shift toward mainstream institutional infrastructure for layer-1 tokens outside the Ethereum ecosystem.
