Pavel Durov has announced that Telegram will take full control of the TON blockchain, a development that sent the TON token climbing more than 85% in response.

The declaration marks a significant shift in the governance structure of the blockchain project, which has long maintained a complex relationship with its founder's messaging platform. Telegram's assumption of direct operational authority over TON represents one of the most material governance changes in the network's history.

Market reaction was immediate and pronounced. TON's price surge suggests investor confidence in Telegram's stewardship of the protocol, or at minimum, positive sentiment around increased institutional backing and clarity of direction. The 85% jump reflected broad-based buying across major exchanges, with trading volumes spiking as market participants repositioned.

Durov's announcement arrives amid ongoing discussion about TON's development roadmap and ecosystem expansion. Telegram's growing focus on blockchain infrastructure and digital commerce has positioned the messaging platform as an increasingly central actor in its own right, beyond its original role as a communication tool.

The move raises questions about the distinction between Telegram's commercial interests and TON's decentralized governance principles, though the market has clearly priced it as a positive signal. Implementation details—including how Telegram's control will be exercised, what autonomy independent validators will retain, and how protocol decisions will be made—remain to be clarified.

TON's ecosystem has expanded substantially over the past year, with growing developer activity, asset bridges, and institutional interest. Telegram's direct involvement in blockchain governance could accelerate these trends or introduce new dynamics in how protocol evolution unfolds.
