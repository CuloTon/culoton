Toncoin has posted a 60% gain in recent trading, marking one of the cryptocurrency's strongest moves in months. The rally appears linked to renewed statements from Pavel Durov, Telegram's founder, who has reiterated the messaging platform's strategic focus on blockchain technology and the TON network.

Durov's comments arrive at a moment when institutional and retail interest in TON-native projects has been building. The blockchain, which emerged from Telegram's abandoned Open Network initiative before being revived as an independent project, has seen increasing activity in decentralized finance and token launches over the past year.

The timing of the price movement and Durov's public remarks suggests investor appetite for clarity around Telegram's long-term relationship with the ecosystem. While Telegram itself does not operate or maintain the TON blockchain—which is now governed by an independent community—the messaging giant's 900 million users represent a potential distribution channel for blockchain-based services.

Durov's defense of the connection also signals Telegram's willingness to remain visible in the space despite the network's autonomous status. This distinction has occasionally caused confusion in the market, with some observers conflating Telegram's involvement with direct control of TON's protocol or token supply.

Analysts note that such statements from high-profile figures can temporarily amplify trading volume and price action, particularly in assets with large retail audiences. The 60% surge may also reflect broader market conditions and technical factors beyond the news cycle.

No specific announcements regarding new Telegram-TON integrations or partnerships accompanied Durov's remarks, according to available reports. The statement appears to have served primarily as a reaffirmation of existing strategic direction rather than a disclosure of new developments.
