A Moscow court has convicted Vladimir Smerkis, the former chief executive of Binance's Russian and Commonwealth of Independent States operations, and sentenced him to five years in a general-regime penal colony. The 41-year-old was arrested in May 2025 on fraud charges after the Presnensky District Court found him guilty under Russian law provisions covering especially large-scale financial crimes.

Smerkis managed Binance's Russian division from early 2022 until September 2023, when the exchange withdrew from the market amid international sanctions and compliance constraints. During his tenure, he cultivated a visible profile in the country's cryptocurrency community.

The conviction stems from a June 2024 incident involving crypto trader and social media personality Oleg Polunin, who engaged Smerkis to manage an audience-growth campaign. Smerkis promised to deliver approximately 2 million new users and requested over 8.8 million rubles—roughly $120,000—for the service. Polunin transferred the agreed sum to a cryptocurrency wallet Smerkis provided. The promised campaign never materialized, and Smerkis did not return the funds.

Smerkis has remained in custody since his detention roughly a year ago. His legal team may still file an appeal against the sentence, which has not yet formally taken effect.

After departing Binance in September 2023, Smerkis co-founded Blum alongside Gleb Kostarev, another former Binance regional manager. The project launched in 2024 as a blockchain-based clicker game, capitalizing on the broader tap-to-earn gaming trend. When Smerkis's arrest became public, the Blum team announced his immediate resignation from his role as chief manager and chief marketing officer.
