Toncoin reached $2.90 this week, a move that forced the liquidation of approximately $29 million in short positions across derivatives markets. The price action illustrates the volatility traders continue to face in the TON ecosystem, where leveraged positions can unwind rapidly during sharp directional moves.

Short liquidations occur when traders betting on a price decline get forced out of positions as prices rise, triggering automatic sell-offs by exchanges to cover losses. The scale of liquidations at this price level suggests that a meaningful number of traders had positioned themselves for weakness.

However, analysts monitoring on-chain and technical indicators have flagged caution. The rally may lack the fundamental momentum to sustain these levels, with several factors pointing to potential pullback risk. Trading volume patterns, whale accumulation data, and broader market sentiment suggest that the $2.90 level could face selling pressure as traders lock in gains or as short-term momentum fades.

Toncoin remains one of the most actively traded assets in the TON ecosystem, and sharp moves in either direction continue to attract leveraged traders. The interplay between liquidation cascades and underlying market conditions means that further volatility remains likely.

For now, the $2.90 peak marks a notable intraday or weekly high, but its durability as a support or resistance level will depend on broader adoption trends, ecosystem development, and macroeconomic factors affecting risk appetite across crypto markets.
