Toncoin has surged more than 100% over the past seven days, a move that market observers directly tie to Pavel Durov's announcement of deeper personal involvement in the blockchain's governance and integration with Telegram.

Durov's MTONGA (Make TON Great Again) initiative signals a shift toward more hands-on network management and expanded Telegram integration. Kirill Pistsov, head of product development at FINAM, interprets this as a market signal that the messaging platform will deepen its commitment to the ecosystem—a bet reinforced by concurrent announcements of fee reductions that could spur user and developer adoption.

XRocket founder Alexander Shepelev strikes a similar note, recalling that the TON community had grown weary of stalled projects and unfulfilled promises. Durov's return to active stewardship appears to have restored confidence, mirroring the momentum the network enjoyed earlier in 2024.

Beyond sentiment, concrete infrastructure gains are emerging. Swap volumes on STON.fi have climbed to over $40 million daily, up from roughly $1.5 million a week earlier—a more than 26-fold jump. This metric suggests the network's transaction layer can absorb real activity without strain. The real action, one analyst notes, will increasingly occur within wallets, bots, and mini-apps rather than on traditional DEX interfaces, a shift that demands robust execution infrastructure operating invisibly to the user.

TON is already weathering stress tests, including recent memecoin waves, and demonstrating the ability to handle traffic spikes with near-instantaneous settlement.

Yet centralization concerns surface. Telegram has launched dozens of validators holding over 100 million TON in aggregate stake. Shepelev acknowledges the validator concentration but argues the arrangement carries minimal risk: validation rewards flow into transparent hands unlikely to abuse their position for market manipulation. Whether this concentration meaningfully undermines network decentralization remains a point of legitimate debate among participants.
