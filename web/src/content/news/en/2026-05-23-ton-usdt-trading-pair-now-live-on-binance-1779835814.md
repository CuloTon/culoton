Binance has activated trading for TON against USDT, the stablecoin pair that allows traders to move between Toncoin and dollar-pegged assets without intermediaries.

The listing marks another step in TON's integration into mainstream exchange infrastructure. USDT pairs are typically the standard for assets seeking broad liquidity pools, particularly for institutional and high-volume traders who prefer stablecoin settlement.

TON, the native token of the Telegram-backed blockchain, has steadily gained exchange support since its launch. The Binance listing comes as the TON ecosystem continues to expand with dApps, DeFi protocols, and native token projects. Direct USDT pairing reduces friction for traders moving capital in and out of Toncoin positions.

The pair joins existing TON trading venues across other major exchanges, though Binance's listing carries particular weight given its market dominance and order flow. Trading pairs on leading exchanges typically correlate with elevated visibility and accessibility for retail and professional traders alike.

No details were disclosed on trading fees, minimum order sizes, or promotional incentives specific to the new pair. Standard Binance spot trading rules apply.
