Toncoin jumped 36% in value following an announcement that Telegram is now directly overseeing the TON blockchain ecosystem, replacing the previously independent TON Foundation in a governance shift.

The messaging giant simultaneously announced a reduction in transaction fees across the network, a move widely interpreted as an effort to improve user experience and network competitiveness. The timing of the governance change and fee cuts appears to have catalyzed the market reaction, with investors pricing in fresh momentum behind the blockchain.

Telegram's assumption of operational stewardship marks a notable transition in how TON is governed. The TON Foundation had previously managed much of the ecosystem's direction and technical development. The shift consolidates decision-making authority within Telegram itself, which created the blockchain originally and has integrated it deeply into its payment and wallet infrastructure.

Fee reductions are a common lever for blockchain adoption, as lower costs remove friction from user transactions and developer incentives. The scale of the reduction was not specified in available reports, though the market's enthusiasm suggests the cuts were material enough to register as meaningful.

Toncoin has long benefited from Telegram's massive user base—the app claims roughly 800 million monthly active users—though mainstream adoption of TON-based services remains limited compared to other major blockchains. Governance clarity and improved transaction economics could theoretically broaden the appeal of on-chain activity.

The 36% rally reflects the kind of sentiment swing typical of smaller-cap blockchains, where structural announcements can move prices sharply. Whether the price move will sustain depends on whether the governance change and fee structure actually drive measurable increases in network activity and developer interest.
