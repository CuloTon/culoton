Binance has activated trading for TON against USDT, the stablecoin pair joining the exchange's growing suite of Toncoin trading options.

The listing marks another step in institutional infrastructure for the TON ecosystem, which has seen accelerating adoption across Telegram's user base and growing developer interest in blockchain applications built on the network.

TON, the native token of the blockchain originally developed by Telegram, has been available on major exchanges for months, but direct USDT pairings reduce friction for traders moving between stablecoin reserves and the token itself. Binance has established itself as a primary liquidity venue for TON trading since the token's earlier listing on the platform.

The pair's availability reflects broader market positioning: as TON continues to differentiate itself through integration with Telegram's 500-million-plus user base, exchange operators are responding with expanded trading infrastructure. USDT pairs are standard for major cryptocurrencies, offering traders a straightforward on/off ramp without routing through BTC or other intermediaries.

No specific launch date detail was provided in the announcement, though the pair is now live for trading. Liquidity conditions and trading volume will likely depend on broader market conditions and user adoption patterns.

Binance remains the primary institutional exchange for TON; other major venues including OKX, Bybit, and Kucoin have also listed the token, competing for trading volume in the growing ecosystem.
