Pavel Durov, Telegram's founder, has asserted that the TON blockchain demonstrates superior transaction finalization speeds relative to other layer-one networks, according to reporting from ForkLog.

The claim positions TON as a competitive advantage in the broader landscape of high-performance blockchains, where transaction throughput and settlement times have become key differentiators. Finalization speed—the time required for a transaction to become immutable on the ledger—is a technical metric that affects user experience, particularly for applications demanding rapid settlement, such as payments or trading platforms.

Durov's statement aligns with TON's technical architecture, which has been engineered for scalability since its inception under Telegram's stewardship. The network has undergone several iterations to optimize consensus mechanisms and reduce latency between transaction submission and final confirmation.

The broader context matters here: as adoption of blockchain applications expands beyond speculative trading into real-world use cases, speed and reliability have moved from marketing claims to operational necessities. Networks compete on these metrics alongside decentralization and security trade-offs.

No independent benchmarks from third-party observers were cited in the claim, nor did Durov specify which networks he was comparing against or the precise finalization times involved. Such measurements can vary depending on methodology—whether counting time-to-first-confirmation, economic finality, or absolute irreversibility across validators.

TON has been gradually gaining traction as a platform for decentralized applications, staking, and token launches, particularly within Telegram's ecosystem of 900+ million users. Whether finalization speed alone constitutes a decisive competitive edge remains a matter of technical and market debate among developers.
