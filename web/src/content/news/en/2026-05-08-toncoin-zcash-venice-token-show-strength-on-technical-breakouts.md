Toncoin, Zcash, and Venice Token have emerged as the week's strongest technical performers, each breaking above multi-month resistance zones on elevated trading volume.

Toncoin (TON) moved through the 0.618 Fibonacci level at $2.74 following a breakout from sustained accumulation that began in May. The rally coincided with the largest daily volume candle since October, and buying momentum has remained consistent through the week. The path to the next Fibonacci target—0.786 at $3.10—appears clear in the near term, though traders cite a distribution block near $2.89 as the intermediate test. If that level converts to support, technical maps extend toward $6.00 for the broader cycle. First support sits at the 0.382 Fib near $2.12. The breakout timing aligns with renewed network attention: Telegram founder Pavel Durov recently unveiled plans positioning Telegram as the network's largest validator.

Zcash (ZEC) cleared the 0.618 Fibonacci at $533, a level that coincided with December resistance. The privacy coin has trended upward since an April bounce. The 0.786 Fib target sits near $628, with the 0.382 providing first support at $400. Volume profile data identifies significant resistance clustering above $690. Momentum readings show RSI at 86, deep in overbought conditions, alongside tight intraday consolidation. Institutional flows have picked up: Multicoin Capital disclosed a substantial position this week, and Zcash's recent Robinhood listing has amplified trading interest.

Venice Token (VVV), the native asset of the Venice AI ecosystem, has posted the most aggressive gains, reaching fresh 2026 highs near $13.96. The token has climbed since a February breakout, with external Fibonacci extensions positioning $17.30 as the next target and $21.52 as a secondary zone. The 14-day RSI sits at 80 without bearish divergence, suggesting room for continued momentum. Support zones rest at the 0.618 Fib ($9.30) and 0.382 Fib ($6.42).

All three assets display elevated volatility readings and stretched momentum, conditions that typically precede either sharp reversals or extended trending moves depending on volume behavior and macro context.
