The rebrand is now official policy. The Open Network community approved renaming the native token from Toncoin to Gram, with 81.22% voting in favor on TON Vote. The poll ran from June 1 to June 8, with voting power set by a snapshot of TON balances taken on May 31. The proposal was submitted by Telegram through a verified TON Vote account, making this the fourth of seven steps in Pavel Durov's "Make TON Great Again" roadmap — and the first that touches what every holder actually sees in their wallet.

The mechanics are deliberately boring, which is the point. The ticker changes from TON to GRAM at 12:00 UTC on June 15. There is no token swap, claim, bridge, or migration, and no redenomination — 10 TON simply becomes 10 GRAM at the same value, on the same ledger, at the same address. Balances, staking positions, smart contracts, NFTs, Jettons and price history all carry over untouched. The blockchain itself keeps the name The Open Network; only the asset's label changes.

The visible work falls on the ecosystem. Exchanges, wallets, explorers and data providers are expected to update names, logos, trading pairs (TON/USDT becomes GRAM/USDT), deposit and withdrawal labels and API feeds by the June 15 cutover, with full consistency targeted for June 22. For holders the takeaway is simple: nothing to do, and a week to get used to a new three-letter name on an asset that is otherwise exactly the same.
