Traders Union has published a long-term price forecast for Toncoin, suggesting the asset could trade near $26 by 2040, contingent on sustained growth in Telegram's user adoption.

The projection hinges on a straightforward premise: as Telegram's messaging platform expands its installed base, more users gain native access to TON's blockchain infrastructure. This network-effect argument frames Toncoin not as a speculative asset but as a utility token whose value correlates with platform utility and reach.

Telegram, with over 900 million monthly active users as of recent counts, represents one of the largest potential on-ramps to blockchain infrastructure for mainstream audiences. The messaging app's integration with TON—initially launched as Gram before regulatory complications led to its community-driven relaunch—positions the token as a settlement and transaction layer for payments, bots, and decentralized applications within the Telegram ecosystem.

The $26 target represents a significant multiple from current levels, though analysts cautioned that such projections remain speculative and dependent on multiple variables: regulatory clarity around Telegram's operations in key jurisdictions, sustained product development on TON, and competition from other blockchain platforms and traditional financial infrastructure.

Long-term price forecasts for cryptocurrency assets are inherently difficult to validate and should be treated as exploratory scenarios rather than predictions. Market conditions, technological adoption, and macroeconomic factors can shift materially over a 16-year timeframe.

Telegram has consistently resisted monetization pressure on its core messaging product, relying instead on optional premium features and business solutions for revenue. A deepening integration with TON could alter that calculus, though the company has not signaled imminent changes to its business model.
