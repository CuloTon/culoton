Revolut has integrated Toncoin conversion functionality, allowing its users to exchange TON tokens for US dollars within the platform.

The move adds another on-ramp and off-ramp option for Toncoin holders seeking liquidity in fiat currency. Revolut, which serves millions of customers globally with multi-currency accounts and cryptocurrency trading, has been gradually expanding its digital asset offerings across supported markets.

Toncoin, the native asset of the TON blockchain, has seen growing adoption among retail investors and traders. Integration with established fintech platforms like Revolut typically signals broader institutional acceptance and addresses a practical pain point for users: the ability to move between crypto and traditional currency without navigating multiple exchanges or intermediaries.

Revolut's addition of TON conversion comes as the broader Telegram ecosystem—which remains closely associated with TON despite regulatory scrutiny—continues developing applications and use cases on the blockchain. The platform has historically been selective about which cryptocurrencies it supports, with listings often tied to user demand and regulatory feasibility in key markets.

For Toncoin traders, the feature reduces friction in entry and exit strategies, particularly for those already using Revolut's existing financial products. The integration does not represent a major technical shift in either ecosystem but reflects the continuing normalization of cryptocurrency access through mainstream financial applications.

Revolut has not specified whether the feature is available across all supported jurisdictions or if regional restrictions apply.
