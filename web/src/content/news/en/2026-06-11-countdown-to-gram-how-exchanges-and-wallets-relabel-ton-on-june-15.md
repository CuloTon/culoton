With governance settled, the Gram switch becomes a logistics exercise. At 12:00 UTC on June 15 the asset's ticker changes from TON to GRAM across the ecosystem. The blockchain stays The Open Network, balances and addresses are untouched, and there is no swap or redenomination — 10 TON is 10 GRAM. What changes is everything on the label side: names, logos, user interfaces, deposit and withdrawal screens, API responses and data feeds.

Practically, expect a staggered rollout rather than a clean flip. Exchanges, wallets, explorers and data providers are asked to relabel by the June 15 cutover, but the proposal only targets full consistency by June 22 — so for about a week you may see TON and GRAM used side by side across different apps, and trading pairs such as TON/USDT renamed to GRAM/USDT at different times. That cosmetic inconsistency, not any risk to funds, is the thing to be ready for.

The one piece of advice worth repeating: treat any message demanding you "swap," "claim," "migrate" or "upgrade" your TON to GRAM as a scam. The rebrand requires zero user action, and rename events are prime cover for phishing. Update nothing, sign nothing, and let your exchange or wallet relabel the asset for you. By June 22 the three letters on your balance should read GRAM — and that is the only change you should see.
