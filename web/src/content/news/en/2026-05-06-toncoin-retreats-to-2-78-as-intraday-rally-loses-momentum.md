Toncoin slipped to around $2.78 on Wednesday, reversing earlier gains as a brief intraday rally ran out of steam, according to exchange data reviewed by Bitget analysts.

The pullback followed what had appeared to be a decisive breakout earlier in the session. The asset had climbed above key resistance levels in the morning hours, signaling potential momentum for further upside. However, selling pressure emerged as price approached its session highs, capping the move and forcing a retreat.

The retreat leaves TON hovering near the middle of its recent range. At current levels, the asset remains above its 200-day moving average, a widely watched indicator of longer-term trend direction. Volume during the decline was moderate, suggesting the sell-off lacked the conviction typically associated with major trend reversals.

Traders appeared cautious heading into the afternoon, with mixed signals across technical indicators. Some metrics suggested oversold conditions that could invite dip-buying, while others pointed to resistance above current levels that would need to be cleared for a sustained advance.

Toncoin has traded in a relatively tight band over recent weeks, with the $2.50–$3.00 range acting as both support and resistance. Today's action reinforces that consolidation pattern, with neither bulls nor bears establishing decisive control.
