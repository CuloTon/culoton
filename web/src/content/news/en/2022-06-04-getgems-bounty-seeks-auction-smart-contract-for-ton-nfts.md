Getgems, the most established NFT marketplace on the TON blockchain, has posted a developer bounty offering more than $6,000 for the creation of a functional auction smart contract.

The initiative, announced on the TON community subreddit, aims to expand the platform's infrastructure by outsourcing development work to the broader programmer community. An auction contract would allow NFT sellers to conduct time-limited bidding processes, a feature that has become standard across major NFT platforms.

The bounty signals both an opportunity and a current gap in Getgems' tooling. While the marketplace handles peer-to-peer sales and collection management, a native auction mechanism would add a critical transaction model for higher-value or sought-after digital assets. The reward size suggests the project is reasonably complex, likely requiring integration with TON's smart contract standards and the platform's existing payment flows.

For developers familiar with TON's TypeScript-based development environment and smart contract patterns, the bounty represents a straightforward commercial engagement. The project sits at the intersection of DeFi primitives and NFT mechanics—building an auction contract requires handling escrow logic, bid tracking, automatic settlement, and gas-efficient state management.

Getgems has positioned itself as TON's primary NFT venue since the network's mainstream adoption accelerated in 2023. Adding native auction functionality would strengthen its competitive position against centralized NFT platforms and align it more closely with mature ecosystems like Ethereum's OpenSea.

The bounty remains open to qualified applicants. Interested developers can find details and submission requirements on the Getgems community channels or TON's development forums.
