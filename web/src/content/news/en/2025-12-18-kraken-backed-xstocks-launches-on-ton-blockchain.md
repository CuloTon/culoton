xStocks, a platform with backing from Kraken, has launched on the TON blockchain, marking another entry point for tokenized equities within the ecosystem.

The platform enables users to trade fractional shares of real-world stocks on chain, a category that has gained traction across multiple blockchain networks over the past two years. By operating on TON, xStocks gains access to the blockchain's user base, which has grown substantially following integration with Telegram.

Kraken's involvement signals institutional confidence in both the TON network and the broader market for on-chain equities. The exchange has made several strategic moves into TON-based projects and services, positioning itself as a gateway between traditional finance and the blockchain.

Tokenized stocks on blockchain networks have emerged as a way to democratize access to equity markets and enable 24/7 trading outside traditional market hours. The sector remains relatively nascent but has attracted interest from both retail participants and institutional players seeking to bridge traditional and decentralized finance.

The launch adds to TON's expanding DeFi footprint. The network has seen increased development activity in recent months, with projects spanning lending, derivatives, and now equity tokenization. Whether xStocks gains meaningful traction will depend on adoption rates, regulatory clarity around tokenized securities, and competition from similar offerings on other chains.

No details on xStocks' fee structure, minimum investment thresholds, or initial stock offerings were disclosed in the announcement.
