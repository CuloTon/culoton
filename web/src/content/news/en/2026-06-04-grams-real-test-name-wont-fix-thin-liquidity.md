After spiking toward $2.21 on the rebrand news, TON eased back toward $2.00 by June 4 — a reminder that a name change moves sentiment, not fundamentals. Durov was explicit that the switch is cosmetic: the transition runs about three weeks, there is no token swap, and ownership records, staking positions and network operations are untouched. The blockchain itself keeps the TON name. Strip away the headline and nothing on-chain is different than it was a week ago.

The bull case is distribution. Tying the token to Telegram's 950-million-plus users — and to the original 2018 "Gram" identity the project abandoned after settling with the SEC — gives it a reach few networks can match. The bear case is just as simple: branding does not change throughput, fees, or the weak spot analysts keep returning to, namely thin on-chain liquidity and shallow order-book depth. A bigger name on the same plumbing is still the same plumbing.

So the real test is the rest of the roadmap, not the logo. TON Pay 2.0 has to convert into payments people actually make, not demos. TON Teleport has to bring genuine Bitcoin liquidity, not just a wrapped-asset announcement. Until that usage shows up in on-chain activity and deeper books, Gram is a stronger story rather than a stronger network — and stories re-rate fast in both directions.

For holders, the near-term picture is a sentiment-driven market parked on support around $2.00, with the $2.80 to $3.00 band overhead as the first real resistance. The fundamentals catch up only if the payments-and-bridge phase lands as advertised. The name has been reclaimed; the harder work is still in front of the network.
