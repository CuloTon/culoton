Binance, the world's largest cryptocurrency exchange by trading volume, has added TON/USDT to its spot trading pairs, according to a listing announcement distributed via news feeds.

TON, the native token of the TON blockchain, is the network launched by Telegram in 2023. The listing provides direct trading between TON and USDT (Tether), one of the most widely used stablecoins in the market.

The addition of TON/USDT on Binance enhances liquidity and accessibility for traders seeking exposure to the token. Prior to this, TON could be traded against other major pairs on the exchange, but the USDT pairing offers a more direct route for those holding stablecoins or seeking stablecoin exits.

TON has gained significant adoption since its mainnet launch, powered by Telegram's existing user base and ecosystem integration. The blockchain has attracted developer interest and decentralized finance applications, though it remains a newer entrant compared to Ethereum and other established Layer 1 networks.

Binance's continued expansion of TON trading pairs reflects broader market recognition of the token and its ecosystem. Stablecoin pairs are typically prioritized by exchanges due to their role in facilitating on-ramp and off-ramp liquidity for both retail and institutional traders.

No specific trading volume or fee structure details were disclosed in the listing announcement. The pair is now available for spot trading on the platform.
