Belarus has formally approved 26 cryptocurrencies for use by cryptobanks, a hybrid financial institution that the country has positioned as a distinct offering combining traditional banking with exchange functionality.

The approved list, released by the Belarus Hi-Tech Park, covers assets by market capitalization: Bitcoin and Ethereum lead the slate, alongside Binance's BNB and Ripple's XRP. Major stablecoins including USDT and USDC made the cut, as did Dogecoin and Shiba Inu. Toncoin is included, having gained attention following Telegram's recent assumption of validator duties on the network.

The decision flows from a presidential decree signed in January by Alexander Lukashenko authorizing cryptobanks to operate and process both fiat and digital asset transactions. The National Bank of the Republic of Belarus and the Hi-Tech Park's supervisory board will jointly regulate the sector.

Notably, the list is not closed. Alexander Egorov, deputy chairman of the national bank, signaled that regulators may expand or contract the roster based on asset volatility and market capitalization. The authority will also consider newer tokens if they demonstrate significant investor traction and asset backing. Some top 20 cryptocurrencies by market cap—including privacy coins Zcash and Monero—did not make this initial round.

The cryptobank framework sits within Belarus's broader push toward fintech prominence. The Hi-Tech Park, often styled as the Belarusian Silicon Valley, extends preferential legal and tax treatment to resident firms. That regime, established in 2017, made Belarus the first post-Soviet jurisdiction to formally legalize cryptocurrency transactions and mining. Cryptobank operators must be Hi-Tech Park residents and register with the central bank to obtain a license.

The move reflects regional positioning: Minsk has framed cryptocurrency infrastructure as a competitive advantage in Central and Eastern Europe's tech economy.
