Binance has activated trading for the TON/USDT pair, allowing users to trade Toncoin directly against the USDT stablecoin on the world's largest cryptocurrency exchange by volume.

The listing represents another step in mainstream adoption for The Open Network, the blockchain incubated by Telegram. TON has steadily expanded its presence on major exchanges since its public trading debut, and the addition of a direct USDT pair on Binance removes friction for traders moving between the stablecoin and the network's native asset.

USDP pairs are among the most liquid trading routes for digital assets, as stablecoins serve as the primary on-ramp and settlement layer for most institutional and retail traders. The availability of this pairing on Binance—which processes the bulk of global spot and derivatives volume—signals confidence from the exchange in TON's liquidity profile and user demand.

The move follows TON's broader market expansion over the past twelve months. The network has seen growth in developer activity, ecosystem dapps, and institutional interest, though it remains significantly smaller by total value locked and daily transaction volume than established layer-one competitors.

Binance has been selective in its TON listings, previously offering TON/BUSD and other stablecoin pairs. The addition of TON/USDT rounds out the major stablecoin options available to Binance users and aligns with the exchange's strategy of supporting networks with active developer communities and use cases beyond speculation.

As with all exchange listings, the pair's liquidity and trading behavior will depend on user adoption and arbitrage activity between Binance and other venues. No details on trading fees, minimum order sizes, or promotional incentives were provided with the announcement.
