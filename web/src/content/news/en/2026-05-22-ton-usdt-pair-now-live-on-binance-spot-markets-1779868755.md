Binance has listed TON against USDT on its spot trading platform, according to market data sources. The pair joins existing TON trading pairs on the exchange and provides traders with a stablecoin-denominated route to the Telegram-backed token.

TON, the native asset of the TON blockchain built by Telegram, has seen growing institutional and retail adoption since its mainnet launch. The addition of a USDT pair on Binance—the world's largest spot crypto exchange by volume—simplifies entry and exit for traders who prefer stablecoin-based positions.

The move reflects Binance's continued commitment to TON ecosystem liquidity. The exchange already offers TON pairs against Bitcoin and other major trading pairs, but the USDT pairing is a standard feature for any major exchange listing, as it serves price discovery and reduces friction for holders seeking to trade without converting to fiat.

TON's price action and trading volume depend on broader market conditions and ecosystem developments. The blockchain has attracted developer activity and decentralized finance projects, though it remains a younger entrant compared to Ethereum and Solana.

No official announcement from either Binance or the TON Foundation was immediately available in public channels at the time of reporting. Such listings typically appear as market updates rather than formal press releases from major exchanges.
