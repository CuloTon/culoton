Toncoin rallied 36% following news that Telegram has assumed direct operational control of the TON blockchain, displacing the independent TON Foundation that has overseen the network since its inception.

The shift marks a significant change in governance structure for the blockchain, which has operated under foundation stewardship as a formally separate entity from Telegram itself. With the transition, Telegram is now directly responsible for core network functions and strategic direction.

Alongside the governance change, Telegram introduced a reduction in transaction fees across the network. The fee cuts are designed to lower barriers to adoption and increase transaction throughput, addressing long-standing criticism from users about cost efficiency on the blockchain.

Market reaction was swift. Toncoin, the native token of the TON blockchain, spiked sharply in the hours following the announcement, with the 36% gain suggesting investor confidence in Telegram's commitment to the network's development. The price movement indicates market participants view the company's direct involvement as a positive signal for the platform's future trajectory.

Telegram has been the primary driver behind TON's development since its launch. The messaging platform's vast user base—over 900 million accounts—positions it as a potential distribution channel should blockchain features become integrated into the core app.

The foundation restructuring underscores broader questions about decentralization in blockchain projects backed by major tech companies. While some see Telegram's stewardship as ensuring faster decision-making and greater resources, others view the shift as a step away from the independent governance model that many cryptocurrency projects aspire to maintain.

Fee reductions and governance clarity may support sustained interest, though observers will likely monitor whether Telegram's hands-on approach influences protocol development in ways that affect broader network stakeholder interests.
