Revolut, the London-based fintech firm, has integrated Toncoin (TON) into its currency conversion infrastructure, enabling users to trade the token directly against Singapore dollars (SGD).

The addition reflects growing demand for crypto-fiat conversion pairs on mainstream payments platforms. Revolut has gradually expanded its cryptocurrency offerings over the past year, and the TON pairing signals confidence in the blockchain's adoption across Southeast Asia, a region where the platform maintains a substantial user base.

Toncoin, the native token of the TON blockchain launched by Telegram, has been gaining traction in Asia-Pacific markets. The move gives retail users a simplified on-ramp to acquire or liquidate TON holdings without routing through secondary exchanges or intermediate currency pairs.

Revolut's support for TON sits alongside the platform's existing cryptocurrency roster, which includes Bitcoin, Ethereum, and dozens of altcoins. The firm has positioned itself as a bridge between traditional finance and digital assets, offering both custodial wallet services and conversion capabilities.

The timing aligns with broader institutional interest in TON following the token's recovery from earlier volatility. Singapore's regulatory environment for cryptocurrency has matured, making it an attractive market for fintech platforms seeking to offer compliant crypto services.

No specific launch date was disclosed, though Revolut's typical rollout follows a staged geographic approach. Users in markets where the pairing is live can execute conversions at Revolut's prevailing exchange rates, subject to standard processing fees.
