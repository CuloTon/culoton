Crypto analyst Xanrox has issued a cautionary note on Bitcoin, arguing that current price levels present a sell-rather-than-buy opportunity as the market enters what he characterizes as altcoin season.

In a technical analysis posted on TradingView, Xanrox highlighted a bearish flag formation on Bitcoin's daily logarithmic chart. He flagged the currency's proximity to a major resistance level, describing the area above current prices as a "huge selling wall." While acknowledging that Bitcoin could theoretically rally toward $83,000–$84,000, he recommended shorting at those levels rather than accumulating.

Xanrox's analysis suggests the recent Bitcoin rally represents a bull trap, with downside risk toward $60,000—roughly 25% below current spot prices around $79,600. Bitcoin fell below $80,000 on recent economic data showing U.S. inflation at 6% year-over-year in April.

The analyst noted that Bitcoin's declining dominance—its share of total crypto market capitalization—signals an imminent shift toward alternative assets. He named eight major altcoins as candidates: Cardano, TRON, Chainlink, Dogecoin, Binance Coin, Stellar, Ripple, and Ethereum. He characterized these as "institutional coins," where large capital flows influence price action. For traders seeking outsized returns, Xanrox recommended smaller-cap tokens with greater upside potential.

TON has emerged as a standout performer, gaining nearly 50% over the past month as network transaction fees declined by 600%. The asset's rise coincides with competitive staking yields on the Toncoin network relative to other Layer-1 platforms. SUI and ONDO have posted gains exceeding 26% and 57% respectively on ecosystem fundamentals.

Xanrox's thesis rests on the cyclical rotation out of Bitcoin—which has already rallied from February lows near $60,000—and into alternative assets offering higher leverage and momentum potential. Whether this call proves prescient or premature remains a matter for the market to settle.
