Arkham Intelligence, a prominent blockchain analytics platform, has withdrawn support for TON monitoring and data services, the firm confirmed this week. The move comes as Toncoin itself reached yearly highs in market value, marking an unusual divergence between ecosystem momentum and analyst coverage.

The reasons behind Arkham's decision were not immediately detailed in the announcement. The platform, known for tracking on-chain activity across major blockchains, had previously provided transaction monitoring, wallet analysis, and ecosystem intelligence for TON users and traders.

Toncoin's price performance has remained resilient despite the analytics withdrawal. The token climbed to its highest point in twelve months, driven by continued development of the Telegram-backed blockchain and expanding adoption among decentralized applications. Trading volumes on platforms including MEXC have reflected sustained interest from retail and institutional participants.

The loss of Arkham's monitoring tools represents a gap in third-party intelligence infrastructure for the TON ecosystem at a moment of technical advancement. Other analytics providers remain active in the space, though Arkham's departure signals potential resource constraints or strategic prioritization decisions within the broader analytics sector.

For traders and developers relying on comprehensive on-chain data, the shift underscores the importance of diversified tool ecosystems. TON's growing technical footprint—particularly around Telegram integration and developer tooling—continues to expand independently of individual platform support withdrawals.

No timeline for potential reinstatement of Arkham's TON coverage was announced.
