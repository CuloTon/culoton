Arkham Intelligence, a leading provider of blockchain analytics and transaction monitoring tools, has withdrawn support for the TON network, according to reports circulating in the ecosystem this week.

The decision comes as Toncoin, the native asset of the TON blockchain, reaches its highest price point in the current calendar year. The timing of Arkham's pullback stands in contrast to renewed investor interest in the network, which has seen sustained trading activity and development momentum throughout 2024.

Arkham's platform has historically provided detailed on-chain data and user identification tools across major blockchain networks, serving institutional clients, researchers, and compliance teams. The firm's decision to drop TON support signals a shift in its coverage priorities, though the company has not issued a public statement explaining the rationale.

The analytics layer remains critical infrastructure in mature blockchain ecosystems, offering visibility into transaction flows, wallet clustering, and suspicious activity patterns. TON's loss of Arkham coverage may reduce transparency for institutional investors and compliance officers evaluating exposure to the network.

TON continues to operate as a fully functional blockchain and development environment, backed by a substantial ecosystem of decentralized applications and community projects. The network's fundamentals remain independent of any single analytics provider's coverage decisions.

The move underscores ongoing debates within the crypto sector about which networks receive priority from major infrastructure vendors—decisions that can reflect everything from technical feasibility to commercial partnerships to regulatory considerations. For now, TON participants will need to rely on alternative data sources and native chain explorers for on-chain intelligence.
