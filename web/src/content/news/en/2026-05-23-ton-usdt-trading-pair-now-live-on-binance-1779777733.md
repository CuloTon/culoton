Binance, the world's largest cryptocurrency exchange by volume, has activated trading for TON against USDT, the stablecoin pegged to the US dollar. The listing marks another step in mainstream adoption of the token native to The Open Network, the blockchain developed by Telegram.

The TON/USDT pairing allows traders to move directly between Telegram's blockchain asset and dollar-denominated stablecoins without requiring intermediate conversions. Binance's infrastructure and liquidity typically ensure tight spreads and reliable execution for such pairs.

The move reflects growing institutional and retail interest in TON, which has gained traction since Telegram's expanded integration of blockchain functionality within its messaging platform. The token has become one of the top 10 cryptocurrencies by market capitalization in recent months.

Binance's listing decisions are closely watched as signals of which assets are gaining regulatory clarity and market viability. The exchange maintains strict listing standards and conducts its own due diligence before adding new pairs.

Tradable pairs on major exchanges serve as key infrastructure for price discovery and liquidity provision. The addition of TON/USDT joins existing TON pairs on Binance, broadening the pathways through which traders can enter and exit the asset.
