Toncoin jumped 36% this week following an announcement that Telegram is taking direct control of the TON blockchain network, displacing the TON Foundation from its governance role.

The shift marks a significant realignment in how the network is managed. Telegram, which created TON as The Open Network before handing it to an independent foundation in 2020, is now assuming operational responsibility. The company has signaled that the transition will include a restructuring of the foundation's role in blockchain stewardship.

Alongside the governance change, Telegram has announced a reduction in transaction fees to near-zero levels. The move is designed to lower barriers to network participation and settlement, addressing a long-standing friction point for users and developers building on TON.

The price surge reflects investor sentiment that tighter alignment between Telegram and the network—coupled with improved fee economics—could accelerate adoption. Telegram's 900 million-plus user base represents a substantial potential audience for TON-based services, though conversion to active blockchain users remains uncertain.

The TON Foundation's reduced role raises questions about governance decentralization. A network steered primarily by a single company faces familiar scrutiny around regulatory risk and long-term autonomy. Telegram has maintained that it seeks to develop TON as infrastructure for the broader ecosystem, though the operational reality now tilts toward direct corporate management.

Fee compression to negligible levels aligns TON with competitors seeking to undercut Ethereum and Solana on transaction costs. Whether the network can sustain validator economics at such minimal fee levels, and how long competitive pricing dynamics will hold, remain open questions.

The reorg is effective immediately, with Telegram beginning direct operation and Validator governance adjustments taking effect over coming weeks.
