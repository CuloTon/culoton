Binance has activated trading for TON against USDT on its spot market, marking another step in deepening liquidity infrastructure for the Telegram-backed blockchain's native asset.

The listing enables traders to move directly between Toncoin and the dollar-pegged stablecoin without intermediary pairs, a standard move for major exchanges seeking to streamline order flow and reduce slippage on high-volume assets.

TON has secured repeated listings across major centralized exchanges over the past year as institutional and retail adoption of the TON ecosystem has expanded. Binance's addition of a direct USDT pair signals confidence in sustained trading demand and reflects the token's maturation within the exchange's product hierarchy.

The pair joins existing TON trading pairs on Binance, including TON/BUSD and TON/USDC, offering market participants multiple stablecoin on-ramps. Direct USD-pegged pairs are typically among the first liquidity pools opened for newly adopted or rising assets, as they serve as the primary reference pricing mechanism for derivatives and perpetual futures trading.

No official announcement has confirmed launch timing or special trading incentives, though Binance routinely offers zero-taker-fee periods on newly listed pairs to encourage early volume. The activation underscores the exchange's continued integration of TON infrastructure as the blockchain competes for dominance in the Layer-1 and application-specific blockchain space.

For traders, the listing reduces friction when entering or exiting TON positions through the world's largest spot exchange by volume.
