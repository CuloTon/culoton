The TON Foundation announced a partnership with Banxa, the payments infrastructure arm of OSL (One Standard Life), to develop stablecoin payment capabilities for Asia-Pacific merchants.

Banxa operates a payments platform designed to facilitate on- and off-ramps for digital assets. The collaboration aims to create merchant-facing stablecoin payment infrastructure tailored to the region's needs, potentially reducing friction for TON-based transactions across the Asia-Pacific market.

OSL, registered in Hong Kong, is a digital asset services provider that has built a suite of infrastructure offerings. Its Banxa division specializes in payment rails and is positioned to bring regulatory clarity and institutional-grade settlement to stablecoin payments in an emerging region for blockchain adoption.

The timing reflects broader industry momentum around stablecoin merchant adoption in Asia, where remittance corridors and cross-border commerce remain fragmented. By anchoring TON's stablecoin ecosystem to an established payments provider, the foundation signals intent to move beyond speculative trading into practical payment use cases.

Key details on deployment timelines, which stablecoins will be supported, or specific merchant onboarding mechanics were not disclosed. The partnership announcement did not include information about transaction volumes, fee structures, or whether additional regional partners are planned.

For TON, the move extends its emerging payments narrative beyond Telegram's native user base into formal merchant infrastructure. For Banxa, it positions the platform as a backend provider for blockchain-native payment flows in a high-growth region. How the infrastructure scales and whether it attracts meaningful merchant adoption remain open questions.
