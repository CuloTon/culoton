Revolut, the London-based fintech firm, has integrated Toncoin (TON) into its multi-asset conversion suite, allowing users to calculate real-time exchange rates between the blockchain's native token and the US dollar.

The move marks another step in TON's gradual adoption by mainstream payment and financial service platforms. Revolut's integration enables customers to see live pricing for TON/USD pairs without leaving the app, simplifying access for retail investors and traders monitoring the asset.

Toncoin, the network token of The Open Network—originally developed by Telegram—has seen modest but steady institutional interest since mainnet launch. Revolut's addition of the conversion tool signals confidence that demand for TON exposure will persist among its user base, which spans millions across Europe and Asia.

The fintech sector's embrace of TON reflects broader market trends: cryptocurrency assets backed by credible infrastructure and clear use cases are gaining traction with regulated financial services companies. Revolut has previously added support for major blockchain tokens and maintains a cautious, compliance-first approach to crypto offerings.

No details were disclosed regarding integration fees or premium features tied to TON conversion through Revolut's platform. The company has not announced plans to enable TON staking or DeFi interactions through the app, keeping the offering focused on basic currency conversion for now.

Toncoin's price, like all digital assets, remains volatile. Real-time tools such as Revolut's are primarily useful for traders executing conversions or investors seeking up-to-date valuations rather than long-term signals. The integration does, however, underline that TON maintains sufficient market liquidity and trading volume to merit inclusion in mainstream fintech platforms.
