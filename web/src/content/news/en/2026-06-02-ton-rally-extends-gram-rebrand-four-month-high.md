TON extended its post-rebrand rally on June 2, trading around $2.16 and briefly tagging $2.18 — its highest level in roughly four months — a day after Pavel Durov said the network's native coin would revert to the Gram name. Twenty-four-hour trading volume jumped about 135% to near $652 million, the kind of participation that tends to confirm a move rather than fade it.

Momentum readings turned hot without yet flashing red. The daily RSI sat near 73, brushing the overbought zone but, by most reads, still with room to run. Analysts framed the $2.80 to $3.00 band as the area where price stalled before and where the next real fight sits; a clean break above it would open a path toward $4. On the downside, $1.80 is the line traders are watching — lose it, and the recovery loses its footing.

The move did not come from nowhere. The Gram rename is the fourth step of Durov's "Make TON Great Again" roadmap, layered on top of a roughly sixfold fee cut, faster block times from an April upgrade, and Telegram installing itself as the network's largest validator in early May. Each step has tightened the story that Telegram is taking direct control of the chain it spun out years ago, and the market has rewarded that narrative.

The caution is the same one that applies to any name-driven pop: the rebrand is cosmetic. There is no token swap, the transition runs about three weeks, and the blockchain keeps the TON name. This week's bid is sentiment, not throughput. The heavier tests — TON Pay rolling out at scale and a Bitcoin bridge bringing outside liquidity — are still ahead, and they, not the logo, will decide whether $3 is a ceiling or a stop along the way.
