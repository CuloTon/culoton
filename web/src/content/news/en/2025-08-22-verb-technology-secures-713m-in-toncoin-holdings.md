Verb Technology, a software and services company, has built up a holding worth approximately $713 million in Toncoin, the native asset of the TON blockchain. The acquisition marks a notable shift in the firm's strategic direction, signaling increased confidence in the ecosystem.

The size of the position—roughly 713 million dollars at current valuations—places Verb among significant institutional holders of the token. The move reflects broader interest from traditional technology firms in blockchain-based platforms, particularly those with established user networks and developer communities.

Verb Technology has not publicly detailed the timeline of the accumulation or the specific business rationale beyond the strategic pivot announcement. Industry observers note that such large acquisitions typically signal either a hedge against market conditions, a commitment to ecosystem development, or preparation for deeper integration with TON-based services.

The TON blockchain, originally developed by Telegram, has grown to support a diverse ecosystem of decentralized applications, with Toncoin serving as the primary medium for transaction fees and network participation. Recent months have seen increased institutional attention to TON, driven partly by Telegram's integration of blockchain services and the network's technical scalability.

Verb Technology's move adds to a pattern of non-crypto companies taking material positions in blockchain assets. The precise use case for the holding—whether as treasury management, collateral for future operations, or stake in TON governance—remains undisclosed. The company has indicated the position aligns with its broader pivot toward blockchain-adjacent services, though specifics on product roadmap or deployment timeline have not been released.

No regulatory concerns or market impact events have been reported in connection with the acquisition.
