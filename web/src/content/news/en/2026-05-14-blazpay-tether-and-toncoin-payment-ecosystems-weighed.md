Three distinct payment infrastructure projects—Blazpay, Tether, and Toncoin—operate across overlapping but separate niches in blockchain settlement, each with different scaling strategies and use cases.

Tether, the largest stablecoin issuer globally, anchors value transfers through USDT issued on multiple networks including TON. Its dominance stems from institutional adoption and liquidity depth. The token functions primarily as a medium of exchange and store of value, with settlement speed and cost determined by the underlying chain.

Toncoin, the native asset of the TON blockchain, serves dual roles: transaction settlement and network security through delegation-based validation. Its throughput advantages—the network targets millions of transactions per second—position it for high-volume, low-friction payments. Unlike Tether, Toncoin benefits from integrated infrastructure: wallet standards, merchant integrations, and seamless tokenomics within the TON ecosystem.

Blazpay represents a newer entrant focused on payment rails, emphasizing merchant adoption and point-of-sale functionality. Its positioning suggests a merchant-first approach rather than a pure-play stablecoin or settlement layer, though specific technical details remain limited in available coverage.

The distinction matters. Tether prioritizes neutrality and multi-chain presence—it is infrastructure agnostic. Toncoin optimizes for speed and volume within its own network. Blazpay appears to target the application layer: the actual point where payments are made.

For users, the practical difference lies in velocity and cost. A Toncoin transaction settles in seconds at minimal expense. A USDT transfer inherits both the network's properties and Tether's operational requirements. Blazpay's positioning suggests merchant convenience may trump settlement layer abstraction.

As payment adoption expands on TON, these three likely coexist rather than compete directly. Each serves distinct market segments—institutional transfers, peer-to-peer settlement, and merchant commerce—within an increasingly specialized ecosystem.
