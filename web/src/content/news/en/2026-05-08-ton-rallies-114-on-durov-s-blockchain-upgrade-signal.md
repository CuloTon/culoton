Toncoin (TON) climbed 114% in a single trading session, according to data from major cryptocurrency exchange MEXC, following a public announcement regarding planned blockchain upgrades.

Pavel Durov, founder of Telegram and steward of the TON ecosystem, signaled an incoming technical enhancement to the network. While details of the upgrade were not exhaustively outlined in the initial announcement, the market interpreted the development as positive for the protocol's trajectory and user utility.

The rally moved TON from its prior price levels into sharply higher territory, marking one of the more pronounced single-session gains in the token's recent history. Trading volume across major exchanges spiked during the move, with MEXC among platforms recording heightened activity.

Upgrade announcements have historically catalysed positive price action in blockchain tokens where community sentiment is aligned with development roadmaps. TON, which has grown steadily as a payment and smart-contract layer tied to Telegram's user base, has seen intermittent rallies tied to protocol milestones and feature deployments.

The 114% move drew attention from both retail traders and institutional observers monitoring the TON ecosystem. Analysts noted that conviction around Durov's technical vision for the network appeared to underpin the price appreciation.

At press time, TON remained elevated from its pre-announcement levels, though price action in cryptocurrency markets remains volatile and subject to rapid reversal. No binding timeline for the upgrade deployment was disclosed in the announcement itself.
