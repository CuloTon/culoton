Robinhood has integrated Toncoin (TON) into its U.S. cryptocurrency trading platform, becoming a major domestic exchange to list the Telegram-linked blockchain asset ahead of Coinbase.

The move signals continued momentum for TON, which has attracted institutional and retail interest despite the SEC's ongoing scrutiny of Telegram itself. Robinhood's addition places Toncoin alongside established layer-1 competitors on one of America's most popular retail crypto platforms.

Industry observers had widely anticipated a Coinbase listing of TON, though no official announcement had been made from the San Francisco exchange as of this report. Robinhood's earlier move underscores the competitive pressure between major U.S. platforms to capture trading volume in emerging blockchain ecosystems.

Toncoin has maintained a substantial market position through 2024, with the TON blockchain gaining developer traction and transaction throughput. The asset trades on major global exchanges and has established liquidity across decentralized venues.

Robinhood's listing decision reflects the platform's strategy of offering a broader range of layer-1 assets to retail users. The exchange has expanded its crypto offerings significantly since 2021, when it began offering bitcoin and ethereum alongside traditional equities and options trading.

TON's inclusion on Robinhood may broaden its accessibility to retail U.S. investors who trade through the platform for stocks and options. The listing does not alter the regulatory environment around TON or its underlying blockchain, where compliance questions have persisted since the Telegram controversy of 2020.
