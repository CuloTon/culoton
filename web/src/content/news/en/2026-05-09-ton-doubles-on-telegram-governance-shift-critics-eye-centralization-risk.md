Toncoin climbed from $1.32 to $2.90 between May 1 and May 7, lifting its market cap to roughly $7.8 billion after Pavel Durov signaled that Telegram would assume primary governance of the TON network and become its leading validator within weeks. The announcement, paired with updated domain control records, crystallized what had been implicit since January: TON functions as Telegram's blockchain infrastructure.

That January exclusivity agreement locked TON into every financial transaction on Telegram's platform. Mini App developers must use TON Connect; Telegram Stars, Premium subscriptions, and ad revenue flow through Toncoin; institutional stablecoins route via TON. The deal gave TON a structural monopoly on Telegram's payment rails.

Durov's May announcement layered governance authority onto that commercial position. For traders, the combination signaled a bet that Telegram's 1.3 billion users could be converted into a payment network with TON as settlement. On-chain activity spiked accordingly: DEX volume jumped 1,054% week-over-week to $152.9 million; perpetuals volume rose 3,200% to $12.4 million.

Yet scale remains a constraint. TON holds $752.5 million in stablecoins against Solana's $15.4 billion and TRON's $89.6 billion. Daily app fees peaked at $1.48 million—a fraction of Solana's $6.37 million on comparable days. The gap between TON's current footprint and its Telegram-powered valuation reflects the market's pricing of Telegram's execution risk: whether Mini App payments and TON Pay can generate real adoption.

Critics have flagged a structural tension. The feature attracting capital—Telegram's centralized control—inverts the decentralization principle underpinning cryptocurrency itself. A blockchain governance by a single entity with 1.3 billion captive users, however efficient operationally, abandons the distributed legitimacy that blockchain advocates argue validates crypto as a category. Whether that trade-off matters to users accustomed to Telegram's existing closed ecosystem remains an open question.
