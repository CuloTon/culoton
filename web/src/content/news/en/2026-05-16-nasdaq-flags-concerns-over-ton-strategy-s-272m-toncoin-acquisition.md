Nasdaq has raised formal concerns with TON Strategy over the fund's $272 million purchase of Toncoin, according to regulatory filings. The warning centers on disclosure requirements and how the transaction was communicated to investors and the broader market.

TON Strategy, which manages significant holdings in the TON blockchain ecosystem, executed the purchase without advance notice to the exchange, triggering a compliance review. Nasdaq's inquiry focuses on whether the fund met its obligations to report material transactions and whether the size of the position relative to overall market liquidity poses disclosure issues under exchange rules.

The distinction between a warning and a formal violation remains unclear from available information. Nasdaq warnings typically prompt entities to review their procedures and may result in corrective measures, though they do not necessarily indicate wrongdoing. TON Strategy has not publicly disclosed any response to the exchange's concerns.

The incident underscores the ongoing friction between institutional cryptocurrency activity and traditional exchange oversight. As larger sums move into digital assets, compliance frameworks built for traditional markets are being tested by the speed and scale of blockchain transactions.

TON, the blockchain developed alongside Telegram, has attracted substantial institutional interest in recent months. Larger positions in the token now trigger scrutiny from conventional financial regulators, a sign that the ecosystem is maturing into territory where traditional capital markets rules increasingly apply.

Neither Nasdaq nor TON Strategy has released additional detail on the timing or resolution of the warning. The case may set precedent for how exchanges handle institutional crypto purchases going forward.
