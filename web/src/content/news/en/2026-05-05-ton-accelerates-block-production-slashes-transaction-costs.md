TON has implemented changes to its core protocol that compress block production time to 400 milliseconds while simultaneously reducing transaction costs by a factor of six.

The faster block times represent a material improvement in network throughput and settlement speed. Shorter intervals between blocks allow the chain to process transactions more rapidly, reducing confirmation latency for users and applications built on TON. This addresses a longstanding constraint in blockchain networks where block time directly correlates with user experience and network congestion.

The six-fold reduction in fees addresses another pain point for developers and users. As adoption of TON continues to grow—particularly through Telegram's native integration—transaction costs have become more visible. Lower fees remove friction from routine operations and make on-chain activity more viable for smaller transactions that might otherwise be uneconomical.

These changes appear designed to position TON as a more competitive platform for high-frequency applications including payments, gaming, and decentralized finance. The combination of speed and cost reduction targets use cases that have historically struggled on more congested networks.

The upgrades reflect ongoing development work by the TON core team to optimize the protocol without compromising decentralization or security. As the network matures and transaction volume increases, such performance enhancements become critical for maintaining a functional user experience at scale.

TON's improvements align with broader industry trends toward faster, cheaper blockchain infrastructure, though the practical impact will depend on sustained adoption and how quickly applications integrate with the updated network parameters.
