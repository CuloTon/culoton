Cryptocurrency markets extended their upward momentum into early May, with the broader sector gaining $17.14 billion since the previous session to reach a total market capitalization of $2.63 trillion.

Institutional activity has emerged as a key driver of the rally. US spot Bitcoin exchange-traded funds recorded $573.28 million in inflows on May 1 alone, marking the highest single-day figure since January. Market analysts attribute the sustained buying pressure to a combination of geopolitical easing and renewed institutional confidence following earlier risk-on sentiment linked to broader policy developments.

Bitcoin itself traded at $80,405, up 0.69% on the session. The cryptocurrency continues to hold within an ascending channel that has guided prices higher since late March. On-chain metrics, including the Chaikin Money Flow indicator, suggest institutional buyers are providing consistent support rather than chasing sentiment-driven rallies.

Toncoin emerged as the day's standout performer, surging approximately 30% to $1.744 following announcements from Pavel Durov regarding the TON ecosystem. The Telegram founder confirmed that transaction fees on the network have been reduced by a factor of six, bringing costs to near-zero levels. Durov also announced that Telegram has become TON's largest validator, positioning the messaging platform as a major force in the blockchain's infrastructure going forward.

The fee reduction carries potential significance given Telegram's user base of 950 million accounts, which could theoretically gain access to low-cost on-chain micropayments. The announcement represents a notable shift in TON's governance structure, with Telegram stepping into a more central operational role.

Looking ahead, market participants are monitoring whether the crypto sector can maintain momentum above the $2.64 trillion resistance level, which could open pathways toward $2.69 trillion and $2.73 trillion in total market capitalization. Support remains established in the $2.58 trillion zone, with deeper levels at $2.48 trillion. Bitcoin's technical setup suggests potential breakout targets above $82,139 could extend toward $86,277.
