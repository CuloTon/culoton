Pavel Durov's arrival as The Open Network's primary operator has redrawn the blockchain's power structure in a single announcement. Telegram will operate TON's largest validator node with a 2.2 million TON stake—roughly $18 billion at current valuations—marking what Durov frames as the third phase of his "Make TON Great Again" roadmap. The market responded by pricing Toncoin to a 100%+ gain in the past week, a rally that reflects genuine conviction that Telegram's 700-million-user base now has native settlement infrastructure.

**On the technical front,** the timing carries weight beyond sentiment. Catchain 2.0 upgrades slashed block finality from ten seconds to one, cut block times to 400 milliseconds, and reduced transaction fees roughly sixfold to $0.0005. The network now claims throughput exceeding 100,000 transactions per second—a specification sheet built for frequent, low-value payments rather than fintech theater. Alexander Tobol, CTO of Wallet in Telegram, positioned the shift as a path toward "the leading blockchain by number of active wallets." STON.fi swap volumes have climbed to over $40 million daily, a 26-fold jump in a week, suggesting the infrastructure can absorb real activity.

**The centralization question,** however, remains unresolved. Durov's direct stewardship ends years of independent TON Foundation governance—a trade-off that prioritizes execution and user integration over distributed legitimacy. Market participants appear willing to accept that bargain for the sake of momentum, though the shift represents a structural departure from the 2020 launch model.

**On the derivatives side,** funding rates and liquidation pressure are building as leverage accumulates. The asset sits in overbought territory on momentum indicators—RSI near 93 in some readings—with intermediate resistance clustering around $2.89. Analysts identify $3.10 as the next Fibonacci target, though profit-taking cascades remain a near-term risk after moves of this magnitude. Support sits at $2.12 on technical maps.

**Beyond price action,** the ecosystem narrative has shifted from stalled projects and broken promises to concrete execution. That credibility reset matters more than any single technical level. Whether Durov can sustain the pace of integration and deliver on the implicit promise—Telegram as TON's killer app—will determine whether this week marks a genuine turning point or another false dawn in the network's volatile history. The next 48 hours will test whether buyers remain committed above key resistance or whether leveraged positions capitulate into the weekend.
